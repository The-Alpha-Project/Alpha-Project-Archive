1. The True Facts About Monthly Fees | 12/1/2003 10:02:13 AM GMTST
First of all I would like to say that this is my first thread on any forum ever. Many people believe that monthly fees are put onto games for the sole purpose of making profit. That being said I will present information needed to decide if monthly fees are necassary or if they are there for profit only.

MMPOGs cost money, lots of it. According to Brad McQuaid, designer and co-creator of EverQuest, that EverQuest costed about $30million to make. EverQuest was made over 5 years ago and the graphics engines were not as complex as the ones used today. A MMPOG made today might cost as much as $60 million to create. World of Warcraft being developed by Blizzard Entertainment is going to be high quality so it is leaning on the high price end.

Monthly fees are present in almost all MMPOGs. Today most MMPOGs cost $12.95 a month; some of the newer ones cost $14.95 a month. Currently there is only one MMPOG with a monthly fee costing less than $12.95 a month. Meridian 59 the very first MMPOG is currently charging $10.95 a month to play. It has been estimated that by mid 2005 most MMPOGs will be charging $14.95 a month. World of Warcraft will most likely be charging $14.95 a month if released after 2004. If it is released in 2004 it might start at $12.95 then rise to $14.95 within a year.

The software needed to play the game will also have a price; most games cost about $50 for the software. Lets assume World of Warcraft when released will cost that much and will have a $15 monthly fee. Now it is posible to calculate how long it will take Blizzard Entertainment to make back the money spent to create the game. Lets assume that World of Warcraft has a very successful launch and has 100,000 subscribers in the first month. With the above figures Blizzard will get $5 million for the software and $1.5 million for the monthly fee. Okay assume World of Warcraft does extreamly well and is able to get an additional 25,000 subscibers a month. In 6 months Blizzard Entertainment would make $30,875,000 just above the $30 million minimum cost. World of Warcraft probabally costed closer to $60 million to make. If Blizzard was able to keep getting 25,000 subscribers a month they would be able to make back the initial cost of the game in a little over a year. In one year Blizzard would have 400,000 subscribers; which is if the game does exceptionatly well otherwise the number will be significantly less. Comparing SquareEnix figure of Final Fantasy XI having 400,000 in Novermber of 2003 after being out for a year and a half. According to Sony in the same month EverQuest had about 480,000 subscribers after being out for 5 years. If you include the ammount of money that will be added and also consider the ammount of money the retailer keeps from the $50 dollar software purchase it will take about 2 years for Blizzard to make equalibrium on the game.

With the presented information in it is now posible to decide if monthly fees are needed or if they are present only for profit. It has been determined that most MMPOGs have monthly fees requried to play the game. It is known that it will take a long time for the game to earn the money it costed to make the game. Now it is known that companies can only use monthly fees for profit only after they have earned the money required to pay for the making of the game and any additional costs it has aquried over time. In conclusion Monthly fees are an important part of MMPOGs if they are to continue to thrive.

There was an interview with Brad McQuaid on 7 January 2004. Brad McQuaid says that because of the success of EverQuest and Ultima Online the Monthly fee model has been proven to work. This model is going to be used in many games to come. You can read the interview here:

http://www.sigilgames.com/000095.php
