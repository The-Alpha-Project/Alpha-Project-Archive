As far as zone areas are concerned...

http://file.imfx.com/wow/WoWpvp.html

I whipped this up in an hour a few days ago to display how typical PVP zones could work out. Each week the horde manages to capture another territory. However, they may only attack one zone (box on the grid) at a time. Same situation applies for the other faction. The dark colored areas are strictly non-pvp. They would be considered too deep into the faction's homeland to capture. The other zones however, are more vunerable since the towns are smaller and the distance from the capitol is much further.

Ok, in the picture:
While the Horde has captured a lot of the Aliance turf, they cannot simply attack any of these zones they want. They have to pick ONE at the beginning of the week and try to get it back. Or they could say to hell with it and try attacking the other faction (if indeed there will be a third). By limiting the number of PVP battleszones to 2 (for two factions) or 3(for 3 factions) they surely wouldn't have to worry about the 10% conflict.

This sort of system would keep things intersting because from the Horde perspective,
1) They will be able to explore new Alliance territories and encounter new mobs since they have captured Alliance territories.
2) Possibly garner new zone specific abilities (i.e. bonuses to skill depending on the zone - as mentioned earlier in this thread by myself)

From the Alliance perspective,
1) There will be fewer zones in which they will be able to freely explore. Now, this wouldn't limit the Alliances ability to enter the zone, they could. However, Horde guards will kill on sight. So, they could simply stay outside of towns to avoid this. Horde PCs cannot attack Alliance PCs in these zones since they are not active battlefields.
2) Consequently, Alliance will pay a penalty of being unable to use the newly captured Alliance towns by the Horde due to the presence of Horde guards.
3) They will be forced to fight harder to gain back their turf. I think this could instill a strong sense of camraderie among fellow faction members!

I don't think having to adapt to these PVP zones would really be a problem for anti-PVP ppl. In fact, I think many will come to enjoy such a system if implemented really well, which I hope Blizzard does for WoW!
