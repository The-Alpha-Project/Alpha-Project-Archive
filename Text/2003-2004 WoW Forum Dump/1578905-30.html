594. Re: All those who want ogre concept as realit | 4/5/2004 3:23:20 PM GMTDT
Actually in a group ogres would take up one slot. If your head is named Joe and your partner is named Bob your character in the group would be JoeBob the Ogre. This would be one external group, the internal ogre would also be a group, a subgroup if you will.

As far as Exp, if one head is asleep he doesn't get any. He is asleep and learns nothing. If your one head is asleep while you are in a group, you get all the experience, not your other head. This is not a disadvantage for your other head, since he still get the bonus of any health you recieve from your level. Internally ogres are on a perpetual round robin system for looting. Once again this is a sub-group, so if its JoeBob's turn in a group to loot, then the loot goes to the subrutine and either Joe or Bob get to loot the corpse.

The warlock summons is mute, the warlock summons one character. It appears to the warlock that he is summoning JoeBob, not joe then bob, So to him he is summoning one player. There would be not penalty such as longer waiting time, the ogre appears on the outside as one player. On the inside using object oriented programming the ogre is an instantiated sub class of character. In this manner to the outside game engine you only have Ogre JoeBob, but in the sub class you have half characters with special features. Half character Joe and half character Bob. They too function like whole character as far as experience and trade skill ect., but some of their inventory is shared, with some is separated, and they have control issues. This is a very programable and workable class.
