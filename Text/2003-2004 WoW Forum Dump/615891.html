43. Re: a PVP system (long read) | 10/1/2003 4:50:36 AM GMTDT
Alright here is what I am getting from this. I am going to make up a situation and see if this is correct. Follow along carefully while I introduce the staff.

Player A is an obnoxious PKer.
Player B is a carebear moon gazer.
Player C is a PVPer with "honor."

Ok, now that we understand who is who let's begin.

Player B is walking around the forest stabbing some rabbits and along comes the evil Player A who so gratifyingly PK's Player B. Player A just so happens to be L20 and Player B was L10. Player A gets an estimated 100 evil points and thus has his new name titled as a colorful RED Player A.

Player A begins killing these furry little rabbits in order to regain his "karma" with the natives of the land. Along comes Player C who notices this vile mongrel. Player C begins to attack Player A and remains his normal color, let's say it's white (just like in Lineage). If Player A kills Player C his/her "karma" will go even higher, but for the sake of the story let's say that Player C kills him. Player C receives no points at all, and Player A drops one item and gains some points simply because dying while red is a penalty in itself since items dropping is possible. So in exchange of EXP and maybe an item, Player A gets some points back into his "karma" as Lineage would say.

That is how the Lineage system is setup. Now how would you be able to stop a L100, who has all the stuff he/she will ever need from town, from going on a constant killing rampage? If this is the highest level possible, wouldn't that give this L100 more time to PVP while waiting for his/her guild to go on a raid?

I am pro PVP/PK in every way or form, but I do like to give the non-PVPers a chance to defend themselves in their weakest stages. This system is nice, but I gurantee by the time you get L100 and you no longer have to spend all this time leveling up anymore, you will be out PVPing people.

Lineage can get away with this setup. The game has been around much longer than WoW has, obviously. That PVP system has had time to evolve and for people to grow into it. This game needs to setup a PVP system that everyone can benefit from. Maybe having the option to choose your alignment from the start. For example, if I choose to be a Night Elf Druid, I should have the creation option of picking whether I want to be a chaotic-evil, angelic-good, neutral-evil, or neutral-good Druid. Based on the decision that you pick, you can have certain PVP limitations on your character. Let's say you decided to pick neutral-evil. This would enable you to engage in the opposing faction, the horde in this case. If the option of chaotic-evil is chosen, then you have the option of PVPing anyone and everyone with the possibility of some severe repercussions. For those who choose to be angelic-good you will only be able to PVP those of the chaotic-evil nature and to defend yourself if engaged from any of the other alignments. Along with this choice will be rewards for following the path of justice.

I apologize for the long post, but I am sick of seeing nothing new being brought up in this PVP forum, and I just wanted to add a little twist to the action that I would like to see occur in what sounds like being one of the best MMORPG's released.
