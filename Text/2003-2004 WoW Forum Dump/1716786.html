1. Improving graphics ? | 5/24/2004 11:21:17 AM GMTDT
Ok, I know graphics are a hot topic, usually starting flame wars, but well...

I'm not the kind of guy who judge a game only by its look. I'm also quite a fan of old games from Amiga or NES, which are quite ugly by the actual standards.
So I'm perfectly aware that there is plenty of other things in a game, like ambiance, style, gameplay, fun, etc.

BUT, still, graphics play their role, and it's an important (perhaps not the most important, but an important nevertheless) one.

And, frankly, I'm a bit worried with WoW on this domain.
And even more, considering that each time someone make a remark about this on this forum, he usually gets about ten "u dont no wat u say so stfu n00b" answer in the minute.
Insulting people that point at flaws isn't the very best method to provide feedback or constructive criticism.
It's simply knee-jerk (and immature) fanboy reaction, which hamper the game more than helping it.


But the point stay, graphics that are already dated when the game is released, is somehow a problem. It won't necessarily ruin the game, but it will definitely make it less immersive, and as such, less interesting.

To have a game that runs correctly on low-end systems is good.
But not everyone has a Celeron 1 GHz from the past century, and not everyone like to see the game quality dragged down because of that.
Especially when it only require to make several levels of details to make it runnable on the low-end machines, and still looking good on the higher ones.

I'm a bit worried, when I see the numerous screenshots, about the lack of details of graphics, which are usually very low-polygons volumes with a simple texture on them.
I'm even more worried about the severe lack of variety of faces (only about sixteen different faces, with just the hair and colors to vary beyond that ? 0_o).
And I've definitely worried about the excessively cartoonish graphics, with enormous feet and hands, and other strange proportions, that make many characters look simply stupid. I mean, I know that's the style of Warcraft, but still it's possible to tone it down in order to keep the style, without ridiculizing the characters.


So, I would like to say : it would probably be good for Blizzard to make the "detail level" slider more significant, by making the actual WoW graphics as the "medium" or "low" settings, and improve dramatically the graphics for the "high" and "highest" ones.

I know it's probably a bit late for all this, but honestly, the excessively low-details and low-polygons graphics are already a drawback, and a MMORPG is the kind of game you play for a long time.
If today it already seems dated, how will it be in one, or two years ?

I hope that Blizzard take this into account, and don't underestimate the potential problem.
