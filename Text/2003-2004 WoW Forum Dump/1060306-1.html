1. (Unofficial) Blizzard Friends List FAQ | 1/27/2004 2:54:41 AM GMTST
Updated as of 1/29

Here's a few things to be aware of, just in case any of this applies to you. :) Just doing my part.

Blizzard Friend List Basics
http://www.blizzard.com/support/?id=awr0698p

(Happy now? ;)


Do I need to sign up for the beta on Wednesday?
No, you don't. Plain and simple.

What if I've changed my shipping address or email since the Frozen throne beta?
You need to contact Tech Support at support@blizzard.com in both cases. IMPORTANT: They do request that you use the email you were contacted with about being placed on the Friends List during the Frozen Throne beta, for verification purposes. Other than that, make sure to give them the full details of who you are (name, address, etc.) and THEN indicate your new shipping address. The representative who replied to me said he was quite willing and happy to change it for me at request. The important thing is the email address you use.

As far as the email change goes, talk to them either way. They may ask for RL details (address and information that was given to them), so keep that in mind.

What if I was one added to the list due to the tournaments in the Frozen Throne beta?
Best to talk to Blizzard Support (support@blizzard.com) and doublecheck that. You should be, provided you acted on the email you should have received about it. (All Friends list members/nominees should've been contacted about being added to it, that I'm aware of.)

Do I have to do anything to stay on the Friends list?
Yes, you have to work for it again, sorry. ;) Look at it this way, you'll be given a beta slot to their unannounced project, as I myself doubt they'll do a beta test of Starcraft: Ghost except an internal one.

What if I was contacted about becoming a Blizzard Friend, but I didn't act on it? Am I still good to go?
If you didn't follow the instructions you were given during the Frozen Throne beta, it seems you're SOL (not expanding that acronym). Only those who confirmed their invitation to the list are on it. I'm told that those who were placed on it due to the tournaments at the end of beta aren't reflected on the web page. For a while, I wasn't but the reps confirmed that I was indeed in the system database for the Friends list.

You could try talking to them about it, but don't hold your breath. You had until from when you were contacted until the end of the beta, and in my case that was about two months. :)

Other Issues
In general, if you're unsure where you stand, the rule of thumb is talk to Blizzard Support (support@blizzard.com). I can't stress that enough.

How can I get on it during WoW beta?
The following (in the quote box) by Hobophobe is pretty much what I meant to say here, so just read what he/she has to say here. :)

Q u o t e:
Basically ... you have to find and report bugs that no one else has noticed, or taken the time to write a thorough report about.

If you're cranking out twenty emails a day, with suggestions, bug reports, balance ideas, and what not, chances are good that you're not spending as much time as you need to be actually researching the bugs.

For example, I got on the Friends List for finding an "infinite resources" bug. Most of the people in the beta learned the "shortcut" form of exploiting the bug, but I first discovered it simply by queueing a couple of buildings and randomly interfering with the worker's path when he went to go construct it. I managed to get the resources for a tower back twice (30 gold, 20 lumber), which I would have missed entirely if I hadn't been looking for anomalies and taking notes on key details.

I also found a couple "trapped unit" game freeze bugs, and a friend of mine got listed for basically taking that bug and researching the heck out of it: what units it affected, how to quickly reproduce it, what the effects on the other players were.

One guy got listed for discovering that the game would crash under certain circumstances with the "Spell Detail" option set to anything under "High" -- which is a perfect example of why Blizzard wants gamers with older PCs in their beta. This is not a bug I would have ever found, simply because I always max out the settings once and never touch them again. I suspect a lot of other beta testers did the same, which just reinforces my point: you have to do the things no one else is bothering to do.

Even if a bug is widely known, if it goes unfixed for more than a couple patches, you can assume that more information on it is needed. Another friend of mine got listed for writing detailed notes on a certain "item selection" bug, where if you clicked an item and then rapidly changed to a control group, it would periodically select that item again. If multiple players were in a game and that item was visible to them at any point, they would all be forced to "look" at that item again at regular intervals (replacing whatever units they had selected).

The conditions for this bug were such that it mostly affected custom games (where fog of war is frequently turned off entirely), rather than ladder games, although occasionally players would complain in the forums that they'd lose a game because that bug totally threw off their micro.

My friend never did determine exactly what caused all the random behavior -- and you could get some truly weird things to happen -- but the initial trigger always seemed to be the rapid selection of the item + switch to a control group, and so she reported that along with her observations. She got invited on the basis of that one email alone.

To conclude, here are some general pointers:

1) Do stuff that no one else is doing. Yes, this often means that you won't be playing the game competitively and you may very well completely ruin your character in a game like World of Warcraft. Deal with it; your beta account will be gone in a few months anyhow.

2) Keep an eye out for recurring complaints on the forums, especially if the reports are consistent through two or three patches. Do your best to reproduce the bugs cited, and see if you can get them to happen in other circumstances.

3) Assume that new functionality is likely to be broken, and do your best to break it. This applies to expansions more so than original code, but if a beta patch adds a new feature or changes some behavior, you can bet that it hasn't been as thoroughly tested as the older code was.

In my opinion you really need only one major bug and one high quality report in order to qualify for the list. You should of course share the minor bugs you find, but don't mistake that for building "credit" towards becoming a Blizzard Friend.


How will I know I'm going to be put on it?
If you qualify for it, you'll be contacted by Blizzard Support. The best way to get contacted? Work for it, and work hard. Go postal finding ways to make the game better, whether through sending in things about gameplay, bugs, or just simply suggestions on random things. Sending reports about all three gets you bonus points, though, since that's what the QA department does (check everything and suggest improvements and changes).

I think that covers the essentials. :)
