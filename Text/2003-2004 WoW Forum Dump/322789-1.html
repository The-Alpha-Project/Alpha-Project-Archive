1. Q(^_^)--O Content (the Importance Of) | 7/21/2003 3:19:21 AM GMTDT
I was reading a couple of other threads about all of these MMO's lacking content. It seems strange to me that all of these companies could make games and not play them themselves enough to know that they have no content. How does this get by quality control or even the initial design stages?

They can have cool graphics, innovative economies, ingenious special abilities, a variety of races and appearances...but when all is said and done, what is it you're encouraged to do when you play the game?

If you look at standard RPG's, the story and content is one of the main selling points (Final Fantasy games being the biggest on content in my opinion). Even Diablo and Diablo 2 had a storyline that progressed as you moved through the quests. I don't think D or D2's story was as involved or personally aimed as say, an FF or straight-up RPG...but it was there, and there was plenty of content for about 30 hours (if you rush it you can do it in less). Warcraft 2 and 3(x) both had storylines that progressed through each mission (War3(x) seemed to put in more effort towards a complex story).

A hack and slash level grind and such, say it ain't so! I hope that anyone who beta tests this game makes plenty of comments on the big complaint for every MMO- and that's content. Is there plenty to do? Is the game repetitive or does it keep you moving and wanting to learn/do more? Does the content aim itself at the gamer, the race, the quest, etc.? For instance, do the quests I get as an Orc differ from the quests I can do as a human? Does the high end game dragons differ depending on race? What will want to make me try races?

Content is what will drive the above. Content is what makes me play RPGs more than once as different characters. Content is what makes people want to keep moving and not be stuck in the same hypnotic repetitious clicking motion for hours on end.

I hope Blizzard agrees on this or at least talked about it amongst themselves, so we don't have another tedious grind to go through.

Post your thoughts...

2. Re: Q(^_^)--O Content (the Importance Of) | 7/21/2003 3:58:06 AM GMTDT
Content is perhaps the only thing that you need not worry about in WoW. I seem to recall (and will try and track down) an interview in which one of the Devs had said that the intial content targets for release had already been met, and that they were continuing to add content.

That said, however, i suspect that you are talking about the type of content, rather than the amount.

So far we know very little about the types of quests that will be available (you can see some screenshots of the 'clear the kobold camp' type quest in various places, and we can fairly safely assume that this is just one of the quick quests, rather than the epic multi-level types that have been alluded to.

As far as getting the same quests as an Orc as you would as a Human goes, i would guess that you will and you won't. If you see what i mean. No? Well, it's like this. As an Orc you will start in a different area from a Human, so naturally the quests will be given out by different NPCs, will be in different areas (at least initially) and will no doubt entail killing different creatures, and/or completing different tasks. However, they may well be the same *type* of quest. That is, retrieve item A for NPC B; protect NPC C from monster D; kill so many of monster E to get item F; and so on.

Content as part of the story line is another matter. Blizzard have said that the storyline will evolve, and that there will be ongoing development and live GM-led events to enhance the story and create a developing game.

All of these are good ideas and should help to offer a range of options other than level grinding. Of course, there will always be grind if you go looking for it. Killing endless numbers of monsters to level up, buying the next set of equipment, and repeating the process may bring you to the highest level quickly, but there's not a heck of a lot of fun had getting there that way. Taking time out to enjoy the various gameplay types available - creeping, questing, crafting, socialising, exploring, etc etc - should be a lot more entertaining.
