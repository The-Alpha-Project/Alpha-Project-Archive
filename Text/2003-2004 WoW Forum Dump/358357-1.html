12. Re: I would like a Roleplaying server. | 8/1/2003 5:53:56 AM GMTDT
clark626,

Its not that roleplaying servers are more real, its that they are more plausible from an emmersion perspective. Basically within a roleplaying server the people would act as if they were a part of the game. The goal being that a third party could not tell the difference between a well made NPC or an actual person playing.

The easiest example would be the difference in conversation content between a roleplaying server and casual gamer server. In a roleplaying server, people would talk about things in the game - discussing how it took them a week to smith their masterpiece sword; this new beast they are taming; how they are having difficulty learning a new spell from their mentor; etc. While on a casual gaming server you might see more out of character conversations. I know some people out of mockery take this to mean using the word "the" instead of "ye" but it really has to do with discussing things that do not exist with in the World of Warcraft. For instance, you might see a couple of Taurens walking around Thunderbluff talking about what they thought of The Matrix Reloaded. Is there a Matrix Reloaded in World of Warcraft? No. Are there even movie theaters in World of Warcraft? I really don't think so. The two Taurens would be interrupting the immersion of the World of Warcraft and thus detracting from the suspension of disbelief that most forms of entertainment try to create.

Despite being more rigid in interpersonal interactions, roleplaying servers do not imply a reduction of character freedom. Basically this stems from the assumption that in the World of Warcraft you play as a character and that haracter is a person. Therefore, your character would have as broad a range of emotions, desires, drives, and neurosis as you. This is no different than on a casual gaming server. Want to kill your friend, steal someone else's items, heckle with everyone over price, yell at the top of your lungs that your the best with an axe this side of Khaz Modan? Well you can them in a roleplaying server just as you would in a casual server. Granted saying "I am Sparky Ireonutz of Khaz Modan, weilder of the Orc-bane Cypterus" takes a lot more typing than "I'm 7331 @x3 d00D" it still expresses the same thing.

Maybe "I'm 7331 @x3 d00D" is all you need for suspension of disbelief, some other people may need more. Having more though doesn't mean having less freedom of character development or a decrease overall plausibility like you make it out to be. In fact, I'd venture to say that having people walking around World of Warcraft talking about their score on the latest Cosmo-quiz does more to reduce the plausibility of World of Warcraft as a real world.
