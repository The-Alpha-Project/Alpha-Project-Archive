1. World of ZooCraft | 5/3/2003 1:18:22 AM GMTDT
With all the posting in reference to Pandarens going on, I'd like to address the issue of animal-human hybrid races. These were-animals are a mistake when implemented into a RPG environment too liberally. Case in point: Iksar, Frogloks, and Moon Kitties in EQ. Exploring Norrath these days is an exercise in absurdity. Wherever you look, all you see are ridiculous creature people, all filling the exact same stereotypical role, and making the game more of like a circus freak show than a fantasy universe.

Tauren are bad enough; however, I can get past one race of bull people as the rare oddity of the world. More than that however, and it starts to become distracting and detrimental to the game experience. Just think for a second how lame Lord of the Rings would be if there was a race of Koala Men fighting alongside the heroes at Helm's Deep. In my opinion it would turn the entire world of Middle Earth into a joke.

The practice of racial creation by way of bastardizing a human form with that of an existing animal is unimaginitive and generic. It offers little in the way of roleplaying and only normalizes a fantasy environment by making it more familiar to references found in real life. It's a mistake, bottom line.

I sort of find it funny when you are talking about originality on a Blizzard game. Not to knock Blizzard or anything... but they are rarely revolutionary, just evolutionary.

Meaning they usually take something existing and make them better.

For example, the other races in the WarCraft Universe are not really original to begin with.

  • Elves are often misconcepted as being Tolkien's creation. Au contraire... Elves have been around since the days of ancient Norse mythology. They were created by the god Frey (or Freyr) and were said to live on the world of Alfhiem (Humans lived on Midgard).
  • Dwarves are ALSO from Norse Mythology. They lived on one of the other worlds (I forget at this point which one) and are surprisingly much unchanged today. Even back then they were creators of the finest weaponry and jewelry.
  • Undead... I dont even think I need to explain this one. Most of the early cultures, and many since as well, believed the undead in one form or another existed.
  • The Orcs are a source of some debate. Some believe that Orc is actually derivated from some language's word for goblin (which language escapes me) and some believe that Orcs were wholly Tolkien's creation.


You shouldn't try to talk about original races in respect to animal/man creatures when none of the other ones are either. I doubt Blizzard is going to change to a more revolutionary game creation style any time soon since evolution has made them so much money.

They only reason I would dislike Pandarens being an actual race in the game is they were originally intended as comic relief. ...I dont think thats needed in WoW.
