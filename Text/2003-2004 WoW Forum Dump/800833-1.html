I've taken Aik-do and Iai-do, and learned proper Tameshigiri. One of the basic requirements for Tameshigiri is a sharp well shaped katana edge. althought its not just the edge that matters to make a Katana cut well, its the whole surface. The blade surface must be polished and the geometry of it must be correct. usally a standard sharpening starts at 220 grit and involves around 5 to 6 different stones. most of the time you only sharpen the last 12 to 16 inches of the blade which is used for cutting.
