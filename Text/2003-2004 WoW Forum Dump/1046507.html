223. Re: Ritual Ideas | 1/24/2004 8:04:44 AM GMTST
I just can't agree with the whole concept of rituals for warrior, and their being non magical in nature. As I understand it from their presentation, they have a mystical outlook on things. Rituals in real life legend always have as well.

Consider that we are in a high fantasy setting, such none magic rituals would also most certainly detract from the air and flavor of the setting.

Secondarily, rituals in that respect should be full caster oriented. Definitely mage, possibly Warlock, and possibly Priest.

Now why do I say possibly Warlock? It's my understanding that Warlocks don't get their magic in the same way that a regular wizard does. My understanding of it, is that they are the traditional "witch" but opted not using the term witch so as not to offend wiccans. This being the case, a traditional witch or Warlock in this case gets their powers from an extra planar entity, generally of a more nefarious power. The Burning Legion as it applies here.

So their magic is almost more of a "power" given to them so to speak. It's not given so much through study as it is a boon from an entity. Perhaps they may study to better understand to some point, and that could be the only justification that I see to them having access to rituals, and certainly not to the degree of a mage givern their class make up and nature.

Priests I can see, as priests in our own world already have rituals that they perform. Excorcism's are a classic ritual of a sort that are performmed per example. While some may not believe in this sort of thing, the actual ritual does exist in the catholic church today, along with many others. Thats just an example. With that being said, imagine what sort of rites might exist in a world full of high fantasy?


As to the bard, I also wondered why there was no bard. I can understand why the Warcraft games would have no bard, but we aren't in warcraft anymore, we are in the WORLD of Warcraft, which is much diverse then a simple wargame. In essence pandora's box has just been opened, and now there is so much more before us then what there was prior.

So will there be a bard?


Titles. I'd love to know whats being done regarding titles. I really hate the thought of someone leveling up, and getting a heritary title of nobility just for reaching level x, y, or z. Titles should come from actions, thought, and deeds.

In EQ early on pre kunark, I earned the title of Lord from my peers. Many had a tendancy to refer to me as "Lord Banegrivm" for my actions as a paladin. It wasn't something that I ever asked for, it was just something that happened through my actions. How I dealt with situations and the way I treated people.

Why should someone that levels up and has no meaning or understanding of the title that would not even live up to it be rewarded in such a fashion? Quite honestly I believe that such rewards should come through action and handed out by GM's.

I think that would be the most equitable and fair way of resolving the issue. Let the GM's watch to see who acts appropriately, and when the events happen, let those whom are involved be rewarded their just do based upon their actions.

Nobility doesn't come from birth, it doesn't come from how fast you can level, or how much loot you have, it comes from how you act, react, and carry yourself as an individual.

227. My problems with other games | 1/24/2004 12:55:58 PM GMTST
I have played EQ, DAOC, and SWG, and I am losing (or have lost) interest in all three.

Back in EQ, I remember my first character, a dark elf cleric. My best friend was a shadow knight and we levelled up in the Nektulos Forest and Lavastorm Mountains zones until the mid teens. I remember the first time we found the bridge across the river in the forest.. It was a foggy night - you couldn't see across.. For the first time in a game, I was afraid. I remember logging in one day and getting a tell from my SK friend who was hiding from some monks in Freeport. I was jealous, because it seemed like such an exciting adventure. Those were the days.

Once I left the forest, the game lost the element of danger. Once I figured out that I wasn't going to be attacked by humans at every turn, the thrill of sneaking around was gone. Then came the uber guilds. Then it was more like Treasure Quest. Then I only played to visit my friends.

When I started playing DAOC, it was a definite improvement from EQ at the time. Melee combat was more involved than auto-attacking. Items degraded and had to be repaired, and replaced as you outgrew them. I liked the way things were done - especially the horses and bindstones. In EQ I hated being reliant on other characters to function normally, and I hated having to run everywhere. Well, the part about being reliant on other players eventually reared its ugly head in DAOC too, with the advent of Spellcrafting. Farming for cash became common as money was needed for trade skills. Drops were no longer the best items. All there was to do was level and grind away at trades. Boring.

I did thoroughly enjoy the Battleground areas with my assassin. It was the most excitement and enjoyment I ever had in DAOC. But once I passed the Realm Point and level caps, that was the end of it. The expansions have elite items now, but I don't feel like grinding through the level treadmill just to acquire artifacts, and then have to level them too. Boring.

There is the potential for PvP too, but since everybody and their brother has a bot account, I wouldn't be able to compete even if I had the best gear. Game over.

Then there was SWG - a short lived experiment for me. When I first started, I had a vision of being an intergalactic arms dealer. I was Terrance Lawless, CEO of Lawless Weaponry. At first I wanted to own a ship I could use for smuggling, but ships weren't in game (and I don't know if they ever will be), so I settled for weaponcrafting. In effect, I also had to master the surveying trade, as that was the most efficient way to find materials to work with. Before long I was teamed up with a businessman and an architect, and several others. We raised money by doing repetitive "search and destroy" type quests, so I had to work on my combat skills. And then, resources started jumping from planet to planet, disappearing on a whim. Frequently, materials would move to sites guarded by agressive NPCs. Sometimes the NPCs would migrate to sites we were already mining with our machines and we would be unable to service them or extract materials from them. Disappointed and frustrated with having to continually scale back or redefine my goals and plans, I closed my account. The only good thing I can say for the game is that the looks of the characters are customizable to an insane level of variety.

Given Blizzard's track record, I'm certain I'll be quite happy with WoW, and I am eagerly awaiting its release. I really want to get into the beta, but I'm not getting my hopes up. Anyway, I do want to bring up a few key points in closing.

Players like to be self sufficient. I shouldn't need a buffbot and full MP SC Alch gear or uber drops just to stand a chance. If I'm a crafter, I shouldn't need to train skills that don't apply to my trade just to get my materials. I don't have a problem with metalworkers doing some mining, but I shouldn't have to hire a contingent of guards and build a freaking extractor just to forge a sword.

From what I've seen, the variety of quest types will be enough to provide challenges to gamers with all kinds of play styles. Simply traveling from one area to another is not an adventure. It is part of the game many of us would skip if it were possible - this brings me to the transportation issue. We all know about the griffons, so Bliz seems to have that covered. On the other hand, being in a dangerous area can be exciting. If all of the encounters are just routine fights... I would have a mixed reaction. I like to have a plan for an encounter, follow the plan, and be rewarded with a flawless victory in that encounter. However, I don't want it to happen the same way every time. That ends up being boring repetition. No danger, no risk, no excitement, no fun. But I'm sure Bliz already has that figured out - that's why D2X 1.10 is so hard. :)

Thinking about hiring guards makes me wonder if there will be mercenary camps like in Warcraft 3... I do like the idea of hiring some goons to watch my back while I'm digging for shiny rocks...

Anyway.. I better go before I overstay my welcome...

Cheers
Banegrivm
