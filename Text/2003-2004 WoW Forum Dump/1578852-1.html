1. Yet Another Ogre Post | 4/2/2004 1:55:12 AM GMTDT
Ok, please don't auto flame me for this, hear me out.
The first thing I did this morning when I woke up was roll outta bed onto Blizzard.com to see the April Fools joke so naturally I immediately discredited the whole thing entirely (but still enjoyed a good laugh from it). But I've been thinking about this all day and this could well be possible (possible to do, not possible that they are doing it). I know everyone and their mother has already posted about this but her's all of what I think in one place.

-Druid Class: Everyone (including myself) has used that fact that you could not have half the ogre transform as the druid class, but I got to thinking and well, why not? Now I don't mean like we're all thinking, where the body splits down the middle and is a half a bear. That's just rediculous. But you could have say, the head and arm change into a bear anbd even have half the body become hairier and more bear-like. There could even be a bonus for having a two-druid Ogre transform. Get a wicked cool two-headed bear beast.

-5th Alliance Race: This one took me a while to think of but I think I've got it. We could get a Gnome-built machine that requires two Gnomes inside it to properly operate it. All the rules of the individual Ogre heads apply for the individual Gnomes inside the machine.

-Cooperation: Obviously the biggest problems (as with everything in an MMORPG) is the attidudes and actions of players. This is increased when players are required to cooperate with each other in order to function properly. But of course that's what MODs are for, right? You could have special MODs that deal with inter-Ogre/Gnomebot (working title) issues. If you are paired with a real griefer, you could report the issue to a MOD and they could first warn the griefer, then, if it continues, the griefer would have his head deleted and the other half would have to deal with a sleeping head until there is another half an Ogre available at which point they would be joined together by a warlock or something. The biggest problem I can see with having the two people have to work together is if one half goes idle for a long time. The person could be E-mailed and if they don't respond within a certain ammount of time, and if the non-idle Ogre consents, the Ogre would be separated and paired with waiting Half-Ogres. The Idle Ogre would be notified of this the first time he signs on after the separation and would at that point be allowed to pair with another Half-Ogre. Now, you won't be able to separate from your partner any time you want to, only if there is a major issue with griefing or not signing on.

I honestly don't believe Blizzard plans on implementing this, at least not on launch, but it's certainly a great idea and very possible to do. I almost want to see Blizzard's designs and "screenshots" of the Gnomebots now :)

Holy cow, I ranted for a long time, sorry 'bout that for anyone who read this far.
