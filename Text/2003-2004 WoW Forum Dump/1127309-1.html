1. The perfect WoW PvP-wise (long) | 2/2/2004 12:26:23 PM GMTST
You are a paladin, and suddenly you come see an undead warrior. Naturally, you want to try to kill him, but you can't! (Original story: http://www.battle.net/forums/wow/thread.aspx?FN=wow-general&T=1095740&P=1&ReplyCount=69#post1095740)

In my opinion, this is very weird. That's why PvP should be Full Factional: whenever you see a sworn enemy, you must be able to attack it, otherwise the wholy story doesn't make sense.

What are other advantages of this system? The commitment you have to your faction. I wanted to explain it, but i saw this post by evilchickenbitz that explains it better than i could:

For my example I will use my experiences on Tallon Zek, a PvP server on EQ. I started playing EQ on Tallon Zek, before playing on the "care bear servers". Durring that time I feel I had more fun and explored the game world more than on the blue servers. Heres why.

First day, in the newbie zone of Nektulos Forrest i got to level 8, ready to try some pvp with my Necromancer. I Stepped out into the East Commons and was greeted by 3 lighties, a warrior, a cleric, and a rogue. The quickly handed me my ass. When I respawned, I shout in Nek that there were lighties (I learned this term by listening to the shout and occ chats the other Dark Elves were having) in EC. We formed a group of low level players and ran into the EC zone. The 7 of us killed the 3 lighties and began to creep out of the canyon and explore the rest of the zone. Durring the day of level on the orc camps, lighties came in and control of the camp changed hands many times. I spent an entire saturday playing EQ because of how much fun i was having, and i only planned on playing a couple of hours.

I remember gathering forces of darkies to raid the lighties lands for coin and the thrill of PvP. I remember getting jumped on my way back to the bank and using all my wits to escape my pursuers. I remember hiding in kodiak bear corpses so i could jump out and attack lone lighties on there way to Freeport. I remember sitting in Nek talking for hours with everyone in the zone, most of which i had never met, about were the enemy was and what zones they were trying to control. I remember all the high level characters hanging out at the newbie log in Nek and buffing and twinking the newbs so they could kill the low level lighties with ease.

This experience was far greater than sitting on an isle in OOT and waiting for the damn AC to spawn so i could loot a ring to get some magic boots. Playing on a PvP server is so much different than a blue server. You almost always fight in groups, and keep your eyes peeled. You make sure everyone is fully healed and buffed before you move out. You warn everyone on your team about enemies you have seen moving around the zone. On a blue server you just solo or walk into a group just to get exp faster and then never talk to those people again (for the most part). On pvp servers you know the names of your strongest allies and strongest foes. You can count on you allies moving out in force when you shout "Lighties just jumped me in Oasis, who wants to help me kick their A$$e$$!". You dont get that kind of widespread unity on blue servers.

In the end PvP and PvE can be described, as far as i see it, in the following ways.

PvP = unpredictability, suspense, adrenaline rushes, quick thinking, and challenge.

PvE = reliability, relaxation, practiced routines, macroing, and security.

If you like to RP, i find it hard to do so unless its PvP. After all if there is a war between to factions how can you RP this if you cant fight your enemy on your terms, not designated PvP zones that one can simply run out of to deny your opponent victory, instead of finding a way to beat them? Not many battles begin with &quot;Would you like to fight today? Yes/No?

Pardon my long post and typos/lack of capital i's.

In general: on a full FvF server you might get ganked, and it will suck. However, you will get revenge. Maybe in the form of finding a group of the opposite faction fighting a dragon, which you slaughter, and then proceed to kill the heavily injured dragon too, for a nice item. Or maybe in the form of sneaking into enemy lands by yourself, finding a lone warrior there resting, and killing him after a good fight and chase. Or maybe from a well executed trap: you attack 4 enemies by yourself, run away, and they will chase you right into the hands of your 8 friends hiding in the woods.

The keyword here is unexpected. When you enter enemy territory, you don't know what will happen. Maybe you will find a lone enemy for an easy kill. Maybe you will find a group that is about evenly matched with your group, and a good fight ensues. Or maybe you run into a bunch of high levels patrolling the border, and you have to run for your lives. This is the fun of FvF PvP; you don't know what is going to happen. That is why the instanced PvP zones aren't satisfying enough: you know what will happen, just follow the spawns from A to B, and you will meet some enemy spawns and players. Ofcourse, this is fun, but it doesn't come near to the thrill of unexpected PvP. It's hard to explain why this is so much fun. The ones that have played FvF MUDS or MMORPGS will know what I am talking about. The others will have to wait to experience it themselves :).

The instanced zones are a good idea in my opinion. They will offer a good opportunity to get a quick fight. But there should also be the unexpected option. So I suggest that both PvP systems coexist: the instanced zones as some kind of "war frontier" (which it currently is i think), but you should also be able to get into enemy territory through the "normal" world.

On to the disadvantages of full FvF PvP:
- High levels killing low levels. To counter this, low level area's/quests should be EXTREMELY hard to reach by the enemy. NPC guards, global warnings of enemies reaching far into your lands, etc.
- Quests in neutral territory becoming very dangerous/opposite factions camping cities/more stuff like this: I am not the right person to find solutions for this. But I am sure they can be solved.
- There will always be people who just want to PvE, and in this system there will always be some risk of getting into PvP. I found an example in this excellent post by shabann:

Hullo! I'm a pathetic, soft-minded gamer. Pleased to meet you. :)

I've been playing RPGs since I first picked up a copy of a *very* early edition of Basic D&D back in 1980. After that, I moved on to Commdore 64 RPGs, like Rings of Zilphin and Wizard's Crown. Then came the PC and games like World of Xeen, and much later came the Playstation and all its Final Fantasy VII+ goodness.

Now, the old pen & paper RPGs were all about friendship and community -- it wasn't about killing the person across the table from you, because if you killed their character they probably wouldn't speak to you for a week. It was about figuring out the DMs puzzles and trying to kill the hordes he sent at you because he had a bad day. It was about hanging out with friends and having a good time. Usually. ;)

The old Commodore, PC, and Playstation games were all about... finishing the game. Killing the bad guys, leveling up, getting to see all the cut-sequences. (Even if it was just a "You Win" screen at the end of the game.) This has kept me amused for more years that I care to count.

As you can tell by my Gateway, I'm a Diablo 2 player. So, I've done my share of PvP. Maybe it's not a real RPG, but it's still a good place to kill people and get some of those tensions out. But, would I ever play it *just* to PvP? Nope. Can't get the good items that way, and for me, that's what Diablo is all about. Gotta love the loot runs.

If all goes as promised, WoW will provide everything all those old games did, and more.

OF COURSE WoW is going to have plenty of old-school gaming. But I can explore vast realms offline whenever I want, in hundreds of games going back to the first Wizardry and leading up to Morrowind and beyond. I can level to my hearts content in Nethack, if it comes down to it.

OF COURSE WoW is going to have plenty of PvP. But if I just want PvP, there are a lot of FPS games out there that will let me kill whoever I want, whenever I want, without having to worry about the tedium of leveling up or exploring the world.

When I play WoW, I'm going to be doing it as more of a social outlet than anything else. It's the MMO part of the MMORPG equation that I'm interested in.

If they have an RP server, that's where you'll find me. I miss the good old days, when I could sit around a dining-room table and really get to know people as I played. On an RP server, I won't be able to get to know any *real* people, of course, but at least I'll get to have some interesting conversations while I game. I won't be doing any PvP, at any time, unless it's with a very close friend and all in the spirit of fun. I don't play MMORPGs to prove that I'm better than someone else, I play them to connect to people, the same way I found other geeks to hang out with by playing D&D.

I'm sorry that some of the folks out there can't have fun just making friends, and playing an old-school game of exploration and advancement. Both of those are good enough for me. They always have been, they always will be. I find my fun in non-competitive ways. I just want to play the game, hang out, and have a good time. Hence, I must be pathetic and soft-minded, as another post claimed. At least I enjoy my feeble-minded attempts at fun.

To you, and others like you I would like to say: give it a try. Unexpected PvP is INCREADIBLY fun, and very addicting :) (Some of you might answer in this thread on their experience with a FvF MMORPG and how it sucked, but when correctly implemented, it will rule). If you still don't want it, I can understand that. That's why i propose 2 seperate servers: 1 full FvF server, and a server where the only PvP is consentual: through duels, or instanced zones. The stories should be different: on the FvF server, the ancient hatreds of both faction towards eachother has proven too strong, and they are at total war now. On the other server, the faction have remained largely at peace, except for some border skirmishes (the instanced PvP zones). This will cater the needs of everyone.

One last thing: some of you are in favor of Full PvP. I think this is a bad idea. The sense of teamwork will be nonexistant. The opportunity for griefing will be enormous.
"But what if this guy is a jerk to me, I want to kill him?"
- Just ignore him, I'm sure Blizzard will come up with something to do that.
"But I want to roleplay a dwarf that hates elves and kills them on sight, just like in your paladin-undead story!"
- The difference here is that you are the exception here. It is natural for the paladin to kill undeads. It isn't natural for a dwarf to kill elves. There is an alliance. I am afraid you will have to roleplay another character.

About a full fvf being a breeding ground for griefers:

First, define "griefing". Different people have widely different definitions for griefing. For example, some would consider being ambushed while exploring or killing mobs "griefing".

Where almost everyone would agree on griefing would be killing noobs in the starting zone. But - as said by the original poster - that can be made so hard that it is near to impossible. First, to enter the noobzones of a race you will first have to venture through the boarder realm and hinterlands - which will be populated by enemy highlvl players. And the guards near the noob zones can be made extremly strong. And he community spirit in PVP servers is usually quite strong - as soon as it is known that some jerkoffs camp near the noob zones you can bet that half of the highlvl population online of that race will visit them there.
So, noob camping will effeciently not present. In a FvF server, mind you,in a FFA it can be a problem.

And about a lvl 60 killing lvl 20s outside the heavily protected noob zones. Possible.
But - as shown is the original posters first example - a overmight in lvls can still be beaten by a overmight in numbers. A lvl 60 won't have much luck against 5 lvl 20s.

In short - it's not like that full FvF will be total chaos. They tend to regulate themselves. There will be PKs - but there will be also PK-Killers.

------------------

I would like to add the enhanced player interaction in server with full (FvF) PVP.

Imagine meeting a member of an enemy fraction in a no-pvp zone on a normal server. What effect does he have on you? None. You cannot interact withhim in any way, not even speak, unless one of you knows the others language. The other player could as well be a mobile tree for all the effect he has on you.

Now, the same situation in a full FvF. If you see him first you have to decide what to do. Avoid him alltogether? Try to make a temporary truce with him (by emotes if everything else fails)? Attack him? Make a mock attack to see if you can chase him off? Calling some mates and attack him together? Just ignore him? 9. Re: The perfect WoW PvP-wise (long) | 2/2/2004 8:31:00 PM GMTST
That's exactly what FvF should be. My personal conditions for good PvP were satisfied, in that:

1) n00bs don't get slaughtered

2) actions have consequences

FvF is the realistic goal that MMORPGS can achieve. Grouping is still essential to your survival in the hostile environments, but you don't have the additional worry of having to watch your own teammates for signs of treachery. As fun and realistic as it is to be suspicious of betrayal, it doesn't fit well because of rule #2 -- if there are no consequences of your betrayal, then there's no deterrent to continue doing it. This sets a dangerous precedent, nobody will work together because nobody trusts anyone.

100% PvP is just an ideal that's spouted around by people who are to naive to understand the nature of humans. If you could kill with no moral compunction or legal complication, would you? This sets up rule #1. You personally might try and be noble and upright as a PvPer, but for every one of you there are 10-20 people that are going to form extermination squads and murder everything that moves and some things that don't.

All in all SOME PvP system is crucial to making a world more alive, if you'll forgive the term. A world without conflict is like a day without sunshine. If the only danger presented is to get slaughtered by a computer that doesn't feel or think independantly, then the feeling of existing a real world gets soured real fast. In playing a PvP MMORPG, you have to know the dangers you get into by fighting an intelligent, crafty, and strong human being bent on killing you. The only options are adapt or quit.
