17. Re: Anyone here play ShadowBane? | 11/27/2003 6:53:54 AM GMTST
I did. From launch until about two weeks.

Shadowbane is a fairly unique experience. The PvP is without a doubt the most intense available in the entire realm of mmorpgs to date. The class balance was also rather brilliant - no one is totally worthless in SB (well, almost no one), and nearly every class has a distinct role in warfare.

With that said, the SB's coding has largely killed the game. Lag during non-large scale PvP situations has been largely ironed out, but client errors resulting in spontaneous crashes and horrible lag during large battles have actually somewhat increased since the early days. Furthermore, the game is totally devoid of content beyond what the players create, and since most of the fascinating guilds have long since departed Aerynth due to the aforementioned flaws, that leaves us with approximately jack crap. The Dev team has also lost it; the newest server (complete with a brand new, "strategically designed" world map) came complete with slower leveling and decrease gold drops, confirming that they neither listen to the vast majority of the player base nor care about their former design strategies (i.e. ready accesibility for casual gamers).

But hell, it didn't cost you $!@% and you get a free month. Play through some characters on test (you can get a guy to level 50 in a day or two if you hook up with some other people) and then try Corruption out (complete with a server pop. representative of the old days). You mind find it to your liking.
