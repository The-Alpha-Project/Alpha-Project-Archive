130. Re: I hear that WoW is same old same old????? | 4/2/2004 4:08:34 AM GMTDT
Greetings Aido, from the posts that I have quickly skimmed I see you are a fellow fan of EQ2?

Anyway, I am "fan" you could say, of EQ2, and of WoW, im also giving an ear to Vanguard though not much info is released on that. I like to retain a clear and logical mind and not give into fanatism

I would like to say that even though EQ2 sure does sound great, so does WoW, and without being a fanboy like you correctly pointed out that a lot of people on these forms are.. I think we can both agree on some issues, good and bad, about the upcoming games.

Good For WoW:

- ability to solo from level 1-60 as freely as you choose, for ALL classe, not just certain ones

- multiple of the same class in the same group stack well, for example, only one paladin aura can be active at one time from one paladin but with two paladins in the group then two auras can be used, most games have stacking issues, like in EQ1 there was NO reason to have more then one shaman in your group

- a focus on quests instead of killing mobs as they come off the convayer belt assembly line. they say that you will have enough quests to complete from 1-60 and never be without. the nice thing is that as many quests as there is, they are totally optional and not needed in order to progress, so you can play the traditional no quest route that a lot of MMORPGs take.

- made by a reputable company in regards to other genre games

- ritual spells, multiple mages can work together in order to open a large portal that can remain open for a period of time where anyone can step through and use. other classes can work together to use big ritual spells too and it sounds great. I like the idea of multiple casters working together for a huge spell effect.

- good graphics befitting the world that WoW is based off from, which also lends it the benefit of that worlds very immersive story and rich back history giving them lots to draw on for future expansion without having to make up "Oh look! another continent discovered!"

- built in lore based factions with pvp enabled areas for them to maintain the feeling that they are races at war, it also gives opposing guilds the chance to have guild wars on the field of battle.

- Instanced housing, so that you wont be littering the landscape with houses like in some previous mmorpgs that have tried offering players housing.

- etc..

anyway, the point is, im sure that a reasonable person, wether he likes WoW or not, can agree that the above listed things DO sound interesting and may have merit. Now there is obviously a lot of bad things about WoW aswell, I could make another post listing those off, but its the same with every MMORPG, EQ2 included, there will be a long list of good points and a long list of bad points. If both WoW and EQ2 (and maybe lineage2 and vanguard) all succeed then that can only benefit the MMORPG industry as it will raise competition and FORCE each game to strive further and further towards excellence.

I do agree with you however, most people here are blind, ignorant, illogical, irrational, fanatical, blizzard fanboys who cant see 2 feet outside blizzards little realm of the gaming world. Everquest 2 looks just as amazing, and its list of good points and awesome features easily matches WoWs, but you wont convince many people here to see those things when they choose to look with their eyes closed.
