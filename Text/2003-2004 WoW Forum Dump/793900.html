83. Re: Dumb Players = Warrior Class? | 11/28/2003 6:45:10 PM GMTST
Casters certainly require a lot of strategy when soloing - but I don't find this comparison helpful in talking about warriors. In most games, other than newbie levels, warriors absolutely cannot solo - there there is no equivalent gaming experience to compare (at least in my experience).

I suspect that some people - even those I play with in EQ - do not understand how a fighter works. Recently a melee class (monk, rogue or sk - can't remember) grabbed aggro from my fighter and asked later if I was taunting. Of course I was, what a ridiculous question - but:

Taking aggro from a fighter is a little like taking crutches from a crippled person. Anyone can do it - but it is never pretty when it happens.

In other words, in EQ and shadowbane, most classes can steal aggro from a fighter if they are not careful. But when this happens it seen as the fighters fault by some. This goes back to the fighter keeping group discipline for difficult zones where losing aggro control has disasterous outcomes. The shortcoming of many arguments so far in my mind is that when we think of fighter 'abilities' we forget fighter responsibilities - and outside the healer (& puller if there is one) he plays a big role in group / raid discipline & coordination.

I tend find some people simply do not understand how fighters work. Sure they are supposed to 'control aggro' but if the fighter excelled at this in the same way he was able to absorb damage - raids would be much much easier.

Think of 'pullers' for a second - e.g. Monks or Shadowknights - who can Feign Death. You have two tools really:

1. Attack
2. Feign Death

Two basic options and yet the complexities of pulling strategically can be a great challenge - especially on major raids (where the boss mob cannot be slowed, snared or rooted). I would absolutley argue that 'pull teams' and 'pullers' have tremendous challenges on their hands - but look at their tool set - pretty basic.

In making the analogy with the pullers in games I would go back, for the higher levels of gaming (not the younger levels):

The strategic challenges a class faces is not defined by abilities at their disposal but by their expectations by the group and the likelihood of a party wipe if they fail.

At mid to high end gaming (shadowbane does not apply here - but EQ does) I think this aptly summarizes figthers and in the same context monks/Shadowknights in the role as pullers.

In the context of WoW, while I expect taunting to be much easier based on Furor's remarks on the FOH website in testing the warrior at Blizzard - many of these same challenges will remain for this class.

86. Re: Dumb Players = Warrior Class? | 12/2/2003 4:29:15 AM GMTST
Okay I am bringing closure to this thread by summarizing some of the main points raised.

What is the role of a warrior in a group? Damage absorber or damage dealer?

There is general consensus that the warrior is a damage absorber. Some folks feel that in WoW he might also be a damage dealer, but if so only at the expense of his ability to be a good "tank".

Whatever you believe the role of warrior to be based on previous experience, what role to do you see for the warrior in WoW?

Folks agree that he will likely be the supreme tank. However, they believe his role to be much more engaging given the different stances available, special attacks and the rage bar. In short, more exciting than traditional games.

If 'dumb players' have adopted the warrior class in other games, will this be any different in WoW?

There is a clear split here where almost half of the posters seems to feel that if warriors were not played by "dumb" players, it was a class that was newbie friendly and appealed to those new to the genre. The other group felt that at the higher end of the game this class was just as challenging as any other class, perhaps more so given his critical role in holding creeps at bay. Ironically, the point was made that if we could generalize that warrior players are "dumb", than caster players must be "cowards" given their role in combat from afar.

Which do you believe to be more challenging to play: a class with few strategic options or a class with many strategic options?

People generally believed that playing characters with more strategic options was more challenging. On previous games there was some disagreement on whether the warrior in fact had as many strategic options or not as other classes. It was unanimous that warriors in WoW would have as many strategic options as other classes.
