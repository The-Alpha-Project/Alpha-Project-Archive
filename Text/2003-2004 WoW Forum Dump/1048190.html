17. Re: ! Killing leaders! | 2/12/2004 5:52:09 PM GMTST
Idk, it makes a lot of sense to me.

Allowing the possibility of killing a leader makes the game much more enjoyable. I really like the idea of storming, say Lordaeron, and trying to take out Slyvanis. The chances of it actually happening would be hideously remote, but just the possibility is enough to warrent a really exciting adventure.

I really don't like the idea of it having a permenant effect in the game. For one it takes control of the Warcraft story away from the designers, and puts it in the hands of, well us. And its been my experience that any system which is forced to react drastically to the events of a select few, (few who have no real control over the game/story) can't work. For instance say it took, 5 extremely high level characters to kill Arthas. Well now what happens? By beating Arthas, technically they were stronger then him. And by being stronger then Arthas, a person who in the Warcraft universe has destroyed a nation, which spawned countless other events which in turn made the game so interesting for us to play. Now hes out of the story, and these 5 gents are what Blizzard has to work with. And lets look at the WORST situation possible,

The dreaded Arthas, prince of Lordaeron, Betrayer, Servant of the Lich King, etc. etc. etc. has been beaten by .....*dramatic pause*.....
PusPuss L50 Warrior, or better yet, someone named LeWtDoOd.

Yaaaa, that'd be enough of a reason to toss the game.

HOWEVER, if they put a little bit of roleplaying into it, you get the same action, but with a much nicer effect. ie: Arthas is beaten, but instead of being killed he teleports out/disappears/runs-away or is barely brought back to life from a very skilled Healer.

Doing something along those lines gives us some Illusion of control, and lets people feel like they arent just playing stagnant game.
