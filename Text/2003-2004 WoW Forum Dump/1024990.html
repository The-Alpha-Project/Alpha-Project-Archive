104. Re: Funniest game moments | 1/22/2004 3:00:07 AM GMTST
I'll add some funny stories to this great collection.

Halo - Xbox - Over a Lan - 5v5 - Blood Gulch - CTF

For my team, I was the primary flag grabber. I'd fly over to the enemy base (in this case, the blue base) in a ghost, grab the flag, and hop back into my ghost and return home. Usually, this trick works because I'm in and out in about 15 seconds. Once, though, I had their flag and was running outside only to discover my ghost had been stolen! Nearby, I spot a warthog. Knowing their tank is nearby and on patrol I hurriedly hop in and proceed hugging the right canyon wall to drive home. Since all 10 people are in one room, I hear the blues yelling that their flag is gone. Soon, one of them spots me in my warthog. I'm nearing the first major crest of the small hills that crisscross the map. Two of their players do the warthog thing- one guns, one drives. Their tank driver begins coming my way from behind their base. As he is lining up his cannon, the blue warthog drives right behind me and gets a tank shot right up the ass. As I'm driving, their flaming wreckage (bodies and all) fly past my screen. I laughed for 10 minutes straight.

EverQuest - 3+ years ago - PVP server

I made a troll named Kazibakum. (Or Yazibakum, I can't remember.) I was fortunate enough to get a Fine Steel Longsword from some high levels that were camping the Oggok (I think that was the troll city) guards. I leveled up to 5 very quickly and made the dangerous trek to Freeport via the West Commonlands entrance. After a few near-deaths in the Ro Deserts, I conned some caster in town to bind me in the hidden tunnel to the left of the entrance. (Behind a disappearing wall.) For a week straight I camped the newb zone. Since I was a troll and had troll regen but had *not* passed level 5, I couldn't be killed by anyone. I dueled people as high as possible (I think it was level 9 max) and beat them. It got to the point where all hunting stopped and the newb players were organizing themselves in militias (parties of 5) to hunt me. A few times they've come close but I always fled back to the tunnel and let the Orc Legionnaire or random Dark Elf deal with them. I think it culminated with high level friends offering bounties on my head and a GM yelling at me for disrupting the game. Idiots.

Half-Life: Opposing Force - Deathmatch - The Train Level (Called tower, I think, or something like that.)

On this map exists a train which runs on a very odd course; not too dissimilar from a rollercoaster track. The coaster car has chest-level walls and a small opening in the back for exit and entry. The car is open air. I got in the car, croched, and rode it around the level on top speed. As I'd speed by, people unable to see me, I'd peck at them with the pistol. They'd go nuts and think they were being sniped.
There are still many that live darkness and must be shown the way.
