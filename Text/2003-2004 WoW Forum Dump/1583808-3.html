44. Re: What are some good books to read? | 4/3/2004 5:44:22 AM GMTDT
If you have a really good sense of humor (hell even a bad sense of humor would work but I guarantee it'll be good by the end of the book) read the Hitchhiker's "trilogy" by Douglas Adams. I believe it's "Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" "Restaurant at the End of the Universe" "Life, the Universe, and Everything" "So Long, and Thanks for all the Fish" and "Mostly Harmless" It's a sci-fi-ish series that's full of extreemly English humor. Just don't read them in a library or something because I guarantee you will be laughing out loud.

Also ALL the books in the Ender's series are a must read. I know Speaker (and all the rest of the subsequent books) feel a lot slower, and Card even said himself he feels like they are a totally separate series from Ender's game, but they are definitely worth it. And of course the three Shadow books ("Ender's Shadow" "Shadow of the Hedgemon" and "Shadow Puppets") are also a mandatory read.

If there is any series that is better than Ender's, it's Stephen King's Dark Tower series. I'm halfway through it now and I've read half the thing with my mouth hanging open. It's that good.
