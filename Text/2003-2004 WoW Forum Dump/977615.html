1. An important thing Final Fantasy XI does well | 1/14/2004 7:46:15 AM GMTST
It encourages new and older player interaction.

The way the class and job system is set up is that you can switch to any job any time. You old class is just put on hold, and you can play your new one all you want with no penalties.

Later on you can do a quest to become an advanced job. When you become an advanced job you start at level one with that job.

This takes a level 30 player, and puts him back in with the community of low level players.

A player doesn't have to pick an advanced job. They can keep leveling their original as high as they want.

The sub job system also enhances this. Subbing a job is leveling up two jobs seperately and then assighning one to be your secondary position. It is much more complicated then that, but it has the same effect, having a high level player going back and fighting with young players.

You can have your own personal opinions on Final Fantasy and its job sytem, thats fine. But keeping the player base circulated with numerous players, while easing new players in is critical.

I think this is pretty important to encourage, otherwise players who join later are often left out. A MMORPG has a peak where it has the highest number of people creating new acounts. If someone joins in the later years of the game, they can be pretty alone in the early levels, as most other people have moved on to other places of the game. (Land expansions do even more to spread the player base thin.)

Blizzard has seemed to adress every other concern I have with the MMORPG genre, except this one.

Will newer players be left out as time goes on in the the game?

I have no doubt WoW will be instantly popular, and actually become THE most popular, respected, and remembered MMORPG of all time.

But there is going to be a point where new acounts and new characters will slow. The creations will never completely stop, but the quality of group experiences, and the number of players to rely on in those critical early levels could be ruined.

I will get WoW the first day, and log on as quick as possible to get into the world. I'll be with all of these other forum goers in wanting to be the first to get a hold on the game. We will be what I call the first generation of the game.

But what about the second? The people who would actually wait a couple weeks for a review of this game? Should we never group together? (if we play at the same pace)

In my attempts to find a new MMORPG to hold me over until WoW I tried a few MMORPGs. These were old, but I figured I would give them a try. The fact that my imiediete "newbie" area was so empty ruined the experience.

How can Blizzard recirculate the player population as I call it? Well I highly doubt they will use any form of subbing or advanced jobs.

However, we know little about Hero Classes. Could it be possible to start over at low level as a hero class (not neccessarily level one)

Also, could there be instances where a high level player could temporarily reduce his level to be able to join his lower level friends?

Times for something like this could be for raiding an epic dungeon. The reduced level player would not get any experience points, but maybe they could get an apropriete number of skill or ability points for participating? (diminishing returns for the extra points to discourage farming of points would be needed)

As of most recently reported in the new gamespy preview there are going to be 1,400 quests. Could a good number of these possibly be open to reducing the level of a player if he wants to do a quest he has outgrown?

Aren't most quests level specific? There are so many quests... who would be able to do all of them for every level they have?

Being able to reduce your level so you can do a quest not only recirculates the player base, it adds a challenge, and enhances the player's understanding of the world by being able to complete any quest they wish at any time they wish.

These are just my ideas, I am not one to just suggest something seeminly challenging without supporting it myself.

If you have read this, I hope you enjoyed it. If you are going to critisize, please read the whole post first, any ideas and comments regardless are wanted.
