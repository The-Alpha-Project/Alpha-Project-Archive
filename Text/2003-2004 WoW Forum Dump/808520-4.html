74. Re: PvP server options: Warning to Bliz (long) | 12/4/2003 3:13:52 PM GMTST
Well, to address some of the issues presented here:

  • Level restriction in PvP - I have had considerable experience with such things. Both with and without a restriction and found that HAVING a restrcition was far less fun then not having it. It just caused so many problems that it just was more frustrating then anything else. Guessing who you can and cannot hurt. Wasting spells and time on people not in your range. Watching as people out of your level range buff and heal the person you are fighting whilst there is nothing you can do to them. It just plain does not work IMHO and I hope to never have to play under that kind of ruleset again.

    Adressing concerns about how "unfairness" of a lack of level restriction, I can assure you it is NOT fair at all when a level 65 smokes a level 10. But it's also no big deal either. The level 65 is surely not gonna brag about his big conquest over a far lesser foe and the level 10 surely is not gonna feel like a lesser player...only one with a lesser character...for now.

    YES, you might think newby griefing would be a problem. And it IS a problem on such servers as Sullon Zek. I often got tells from lowbies on my team asking us to come kill a high level evil or goody griefing in our newby zones. Sometimes I could help and sometimes I was busy. This, however, is where Sony goofed up and Blizzard can correct the mistake.

    If Bliz was to make guards faction unexploitable (meaning guards would attack different factions on sight), it would go a long ways to resolving newby griefing. The trouble with Sullon Zek is high level would change their factiona nd often have BETTER faction then the newbies in their own citites. A higher level griefer could then sit there and have a field day. In fact, it would often hard to get the girefer out of the city because your OWN GUARDS would attack you for attacking HIM!

    This was a HUGE HUGE HUGE HUGE HUGE problem on Sullon Zek...and perhaps one of the biggest aside from the retarded team balance. If Bliz makes guards in newby areas that are tough and not exploitable....newbies will still see some action, but it won't be from griefers just sitting in their newby zone all day long preventing them from leveling and learning the game.


    • Penalty/Reward for PvP death/Victory - I really think this is up in the air. I kinda prefer a no item loot situation so folks do not bag or bank everything or not wear stuff. I believe there needs to be a penalty for death in PvP so getting PvP killed is not meaningless (within a given level restriction). If a super high level kills a lowby, however, no penalty for death will happen to the lowby.

    Some other suggestions
    1) Don't make non-pvp zones like the bazaar/nexus/pot. This also opens up a black market for trading items between teams. If I am an orc selling orccraftedblade005 to a member of the alliance, I should always be worried that he is going to try and kill me.

    2) Take steps to prevent training. Perhaps you could limit the duration of movement increasing effects through the use of stamina in order to sprint/move faster than creatures. Have resurection consent boxes, teleportation consent boxes, and invulnerability effects disappear when characters move. Detect and severely punish people using speed hacks.

    3) Support a points board. I really enjoyed looking at the changes in the sullon zek zone control maps at the beginning of the server. However, the points board is broken, the maps haven't been updated with new zones from the 3 or 4 expansion since they were created, and there are no rewards for insignias.
    The summon corpse potions are just a plug to fix the broken team balance on the server. Perhaps killing members of other teams could give you points which could be redeemed for less experience than the opponent lost or quest items to upgrade your gear. This allows individuals who enjoy pvping to progress their character w/o playing the blue game. They should progress slower, but still be reward for their service to cleaning the lands of the horde/alliance.

    4) Try to limit the impact of locked doors, zones requiring flags, and instanced dungeons. The other two teams on sullon really didn't have a chance once evil team got behind locked doors/protected zones. Evil team could then choose when to pvp and when to pve. No other teams had that option and could be harassed at any time. Being able to choose when and where you will fight every battle is a big advantage.
