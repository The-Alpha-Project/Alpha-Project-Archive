I don't know if I'm allowed to post it or not, but one of the fansites posted the TOS from the leaked alpha. THere was some interesting information in it. I'll summarize:

1) Kiting monsters to unsuspecting players is a bannable offense.

2) Kill stealing is a bannable offense.

3) Using the in-game chat to advertise your intent to sell your account is a bannable offense.

4) Selling your account is perfectly fine -- so long as it's not done within the WoW servers themselves (i.e. through some alternate channel like ebay). It does specifically say that the transferring of accounts amongst players is not "regulated by Blizzad" and is thus "OK" as long as it's done without being advertised in the in-game chat channels. So ebay is perfectly fine.

5) Apparently selling items is not ok -- only entire accounts. But this doens't make much sense to me...not sure if this is really what Blizzard meant.

6) Theres one rule which is super vauge: "Acting in a manner contrary to the 'essence of World of Warcraft' is a bannable offense." This rule is way too vauge. A potentially valid interpretation of it is that you have to "remain in character". You could, theoretically with the wording of that rule, be banned for just about anything.g . .

Say for a minute that you have two guys.

One is on disability and is unable to work for the remainder of his life. This gives him ample time to do the things he likes. Lets say playing MMORPGs among other things.

On the other hand, we have a guy who works as an Armed Guard for an armored cash truck. Lets say playing MMORPGs among other things.

Anyway, just my thoughts. I try to think about both sides of the situation.

32. Re: What is blizzards plan on ebayers? | 2/3/2004 3:24:32 AM GMTST
Ebaying is NOTHING like duping or hacking.

There remains only one, legitimate item. It's simply changed owners.

People can GIVE items to each other, for free. Sometimes because they like them, sometimes for favours. Sometimes guilds keep huge amounts of great items on hand, to DONATE to new members, or whoever needs them.

People can SELL items to each other for gold, or TRADE them for items.

Please specify EXACTLY how an ebayed item differs from one that is given, donated, sold, or traded.

Inflation is a result of too much value or currency entering the economy. Ebaying doesn't create any value or items; it simply moves them around between consenting players. The Ebayed item was found by a player who took the time to hunt monsters for it, or crafted it.

Some people simply have more money than time. What an Ebay trade is about, is one person trading the real-world money they've earned, for someone else's real time.

Example of the above sentence:
-You have one powerful character (because a lot of time was invested in him/her) with a lot of power in terms of money, items, etc.

-You have one weak character (because little time was invested in him/her) with little power in terms of money, items, etc.

Now the weak wants something from the strong. The weak can either:
a) Obtain the desired item via in game trade thus no shift in power (because the strong got a monetary return)
b) Obtain the item via Ebay shifting some of the power to the weaker character (because the weak retained his belongings/money AND acquired the desired item).

Does this apply to every situation? No. But I'm sure it does at times.
