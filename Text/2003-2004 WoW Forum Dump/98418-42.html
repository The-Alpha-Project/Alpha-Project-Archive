828. Re: Naming policy | 8/1/2003 11:06:40 AM GMTDT
I don't think they should add a too strict naming policy. I don't mind banning names with numbers and weird symbols like %&$ and common names like "Gandalf", but names like "Chaos-Grom" should be allowed. Not everyone plays MMORPGs to "roleplay", in fact the *true* roleplayers are imho the minority. I played Ultima Online for like one year and DaoC for about 5 month so I know what I'm talking about. I never roleplayed and probably never will, I play MMORPGs to relax, have fun, explore the world and collect virtual items + strong characters. I also don't want to be restricted with selling these characters and items via ebay, I traded lots of virtual stuff in the past and earned my monthly membership-fee back by doing so. I don't want to care about insane rp-rules, when making a new character I always "powergame" him/her and don't want to waste time by roleplaying. I of course respect people who have different gaming-styles. The game should be open to everyone. If you don't like d00ds or a specific kind of gamers, use the /ignore-command which will surely exist in the game or join a rp-only-guild. Most of the d00ds usually disappear after the first month anyway because they got bored or can't afford the monthly $15 (most of the d00ds aren't adults). You can't just "forbit" specific kinds of people, as long as they don't harm you by having an offensive name or insulting everyone, they aren't a problem. In real life there are strange persons, too. Strict RP-rules are like demanding to remove gay people because they don't "fit" into the society and ruin the mood... Have you ever thought about that some roleplayers could ruin the "mood" for the so-called "d00ds" who prefer a chaotic world, aswell?

Names are an important aspect of someones personality and they add a lot of individuality. Names like "Loeikeas Aemspear" or "Meamreo Neasot" all sound the same to me and I find them boring. I surely would have problems remembering about 50 of these names when beeing member of a guild, but it is easy to remember names like "IronFish" or "GreedyCactus". I would rather party with someone who has an original name than someone who is called "Quszo Lojen".

I would suggest making a RP-server where all these hardcore-roleplayers who would even like to ban OOC-chat could go instead of forcing customers who pay money every month to take a name they don't like. Remember that games are made for the wide public and not for just a specific kind of gamers. I believe that most game-designers focus on the "extreme" gamers (this includes the true RPGers, people who are online 24/7 and gosus) too much, anyway. Remember that the most customers are still causal gamers who aren't online so often and just want to have fun, without beeing afraid of breaking some rule every 5 minutes. They pay the most money, they are the majority (even though the extreme gamers are more active and have a stronger voice, for example here at these forums, because the causal gamers usually don't care about posting; most of the future WoW-customers don't even know what WoW means yet, I presume) and the game should be designed to please them.

Just my 2 cent.
