I would like to see Blizzard continue as they have with the previous level of PVP involvment in previous games they have developed. Freedom of choice regarding PVP combat outside of the cities? Yes.

Freedom to slaughter new players in the starting areas? No.

Combat amoung players is important... not only for the game's setting and environment... but for the gameplay experience of the players.

229. Re: Poll: Would you play on a faction PvP server | 10/29/2003 2:06:19 AM GMTST
Q u o t e:
No. I don't find that all to interesting. I'm in for the roleplaying, adventuring and questing - experiencing the World of Warcraft. Though that could be done on a PvP server too, I wouldn't think many on that server would be interested in that.
I am still wondering how you can say that you will roleplay in the World of WarCraft without being interested in PvP against the other faction. To play the role of almost any character in this game will imply the serious urge to kill members of the opposing faction, so how can you separate the two? Ill reiterate what I stated earlier: factional PvP will protect against the ganking of new characters, but will not impose a frustrating and artificial limitation upon roleplaying. I would also suggest that part of experiencing the World of WarCraft will be the experience of fighting the other faction.
Q u o t e:
No, those servers are not all that popular in EQ. I have been playing EQ for 3 years, and it's all about meeting new people, adventuring/seeing new places, and getting together and killing large bosses. Once you start playing this type of game you realize that open PvP is wrong in this sort of experience. PvP is best left to certain areas like arenas.
Ok, now I could be wrong about this, because my EQ experience consists of about 2 hours, 4 years or so ago. My understanding, however, is that every character in EQ is, more-or-less, on the same side. Any character can, for example, group with any other character. Every race can communicate with every other race. Every race is, in a broad sense, good guys.

World of WarCraft, by contrast, is the exact opposite. Whatever your race, there will be other races who are, from your perspective, the bad guys. You cant (for the most part) talk to them, you cant join them in a party, you cant shop in their towns they are the enemy. Comparing this game, and PvP within it, to EQ is like comparing it to Diablo II they are just nothing alike. PvP in D2 is retarded, because what you have is just combat among the good guys it has NOTHING to do with the plot, or the dynamics of the game world.

Here in the World of WarCraft, you have the races divided into factions which have, except for brief interludes, always been at war with one another. If there is a way to make a game more perfectly designed for factional PvP, I cant think of it at the moment.

Nothing about having factional PvP will limit your ability to meet new people, because anyone you can talk with will not be from the other faction, anyway. Nothing will get in the way of adventuring, and youll not likely come into contact with members of the other faction until you have completed a fair number of quests, and racked up a few levels, anyway because starting off, youll be playing in and around a city full of people of your own faction.

I am not in favor of factional PvP because I like to fight with other players, I am for it because I think it is necessary for the World to make sense, necessary for roleplaying. I never want to be in the situation where my Paladin has to say Oh, these zombies, we can kill, but those over there we cant. Those must be players. When I am playing, I dont want to have to make distinctions between computer controlled bad guys and player controlled bad guys.
