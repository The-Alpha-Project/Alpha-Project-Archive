Now to the people who are posting that it should be a dollar. While I'd personally love to see this (if it wouldn't make them file Chapter 11), it is absolutely pure and total FANTASY. The more players the better, more players = more money for Blizzard, both in sales and fees. Here is the problem, it is called "point of diminishing returns". For every X number of suscribers Blizzard has to add additional servers, bandwidth, and support. So if they only charged a $1 and got a billion users they'd be making a $1 billion a month. How much is it costing for all this? There will come a point where cost will meet and exceed income. I liked the post listing some of the costs, but they neglected to mention that the production is more than paid for by the box sales. So the $62K he mentioned per month after all else is said and done is quite good. Most business don't run that kind of profit margin.
