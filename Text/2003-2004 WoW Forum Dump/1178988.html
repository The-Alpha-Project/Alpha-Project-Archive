423. Re: Just Curious | 2/10/2004 5:54:06 PM GMTST
My MMORPG past isn't too extensive. I've played games for over 20 years, but I always avoided MMORPG's... They just never caught my attention I guess.

My spare time is like gold to me, so I find myself becoming more and more picky about what I do with it.


What I look for:

PvP:
I LOVE full PvP. LOVE IT. Any MMORPG who doesn't include this in a game is limiting their audience AND they are limiting their customers options on how they play. World of Warcraft is no different. If you have to put us PvP "animals" on our own server, so be it, but you MUST include full PvP on at least a couple of servers. I think it ALL comes down to human nature. People COMPETE. plain and simple. Competition. Why has online game-playing flourished so much? COMPETITION. We WANT to compete. We want to fight other people. We want to be better sometimes, or perhaps worse sometimes. Even the tamer PvE and Crafting only types will tell you, competition drives them. A crafters competing is done in the marketplace. They compete with countless others for a share of the market. The wealthiest players are the best at what they do, and they'll tell you so. PvEers compete too. You'll hear it in every conversation in game... "I just hit that mob for 120hp!" "Watch this spell though, it does 140hp!"

Check in template forums... Why do so many people create character "templates"? COMPETITION! "My character build can do more damage than yours" "Well, mine's better at defense"

Who are we trying to be better than? Everyone else, that's who. Or at least a majority of them. Competition allows people smaller moments of glory. No competition? I promise, 99% of the time when a person quits a MMORPG it's because they seen the end. They've seen where they're headed... "Great... I'm almost level 100 and I can't go any higher than that... I've got all the best armor... I've got the best weapon... Hell, I've got the best character... But WHY? I can't use it for anything other than hitting Lord Uber-Rat over there..."

Full PvP extends the life of an MMORPG. WAY beyond what a PvE only game can expect... It's about the END GAME. Telling someone they can log on every night for some Arena PvP, or zone PvP will only drive that person off. In a full PvP setting, once I reach the top, guess what? I haven't... There MIGHT be someone out there that can beat me...

OK, enough on that...

Crafting:
I look for ONE thing in crafting... Diversity... If you limit my choices as to what I can do, I won't even mess with the system. I want a Warrior who can craft his own armor, his own weapon, his potions... If I can only build weapons? Not interested. I'm an adventurer by nature. I'd rather hack away at a dragon than hack away at a pile of rock. If the skill has no use for my character, then I have no use for it. That's just me though... I can understand limiting crafting, but in systems like that I think "Well, since I'm already going to be spending gold on the things I can't build I may as well not waste 100 hours of my life becoming a master blacksmith. I'll just kill a little more to get my money and I'll buy EVERYTHING I need."

Land Ownership:
Land development... WoW isn't planning on having it, and in my eyes that's a huge blemish. Even among the MMORPGS out there now, in their sad beta-like shape and poor gameplay experience, most have a land-ownership and development system in place.

It could be that WoW will be fun enough without it, but you're once again talking about end game here. People WILL reach the level cap. QUICKLY. If there's nothing for them to do up there, it's e-bay for their account and they're on their way. There MUST be something to do after the crafting and leveling have stopped. I say that you guys (Blizzard) ignore that point at your own peril. Not including full PvP AND omitting land development means there's very little to do at the top. Quests are awesome, but what's the point once you hit the top? More money? For what? the man at the top doesn't need equipment... He already has it... He doesn't need money... He's got nothing to spend it on...

Chat/Grouping system:
Interaction is the reason many folks play. What seperates a MMORPG from it's single player counterparts is one BIG gap... In a MMORPG I can talk to 1000 other people, playing the game like me, sitting at their keyboard like me... If I was just after equipment/money/fame, I could go play a regular RPG. I want INTERACTION. I want the human touch to my virtual worlds. I know that the second time through a regular RPG, that NPC is going to tell me the same damn thing he told me last time. In a MMORPG, I never know if this guy walking by on the road is going to say "Hi" or ask me a question or lead me off to fight with him or ASSAULT me for my money. I want a very intuitive, easily navigable chat system where I can communicate with the people around me.

Guilds:
Guilds are what they are. There's really no need for re-invention because they've been around in many forms since the beginning of man. An easy communication channel, a bulletin board of some type, and a way to arrange my people into tiers, and I'm happy.


So, I'm stopping here. Rambled on too much, i suppose... But if you wanted information, or a peek into the head of one of your many fans, there it is. Information is powerful stuff...
