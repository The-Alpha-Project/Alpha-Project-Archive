1. +_+ Semi Solution To PvP +_+ | 11/10/2003 7:31:24 AM GMTST
Here is what we know. There will be PvP. It will stay true to War Craft 3(factional PvP). It will be regulated.

Now my proprosal is a bit of a read, so bare with me.

Normal PvP would be any Alliance can fight any Horde basically anywhere. But most PvP would take place in a no-mans land. Basically a Line between the two armies. If a player somhow passes through one of these battle zones without getting ganked; which would be large, warring-NPC-filled battle zones with keeps, camps, and a whole lot of fighting, the player can be attacked by anyone or anything(Ally NPCs). He can attack Players as he wishes but the area would be full of only Alliance unlike the battle zone where things are much more even.
To protect low lvl players there will be safe zones. In the safe zones the enemy Horde may not attack any players but they can attack him. Basically those safe can run around him and he cant do anything but if they choose to attack him he can fight back. But still be weary of enemy NPC's and high lvl players.

Back to the Line of Battle. The Line should be ever changing. As one faction pushes forward killing NPCs and taking keeps and other vital places the line shifts. Eventually shifting over a town.
I would love to see a large battle over a town. Players vs NPCs and players. Winning the town could be as simple as killing all the Alliance Guards, Killing all the Players, having the kill the govenor/king/leader of the town, reaching an item or a flag etc etc etc.

Winning the town would not only give a foothold into enemy territory(supplies, potions, weapons all right there). But the Battle zone would move all the way over the town.

It should be very hard to move the line and it move slowly so that it take a very long time to reach a capital(taking hundreds of fully lvld characters to dent).

My point in taking capitols is there has to be a goal for PvP. It cant just be gank and gank forever. No point = no fun = no motivation in the long run. Sure its fun for a short time, but it gets old and borring. But with an ultimate purpose it gives players one more thing to strive for(even if it is nearly or basically impossible).

Please tweak, critisise and flame away!

4. Re: +_+ Semi Solution To PvP +_+ | 11/14/2003 7:37:14 AM GMTST
Great ideas. Here are a few of mine.

After a border town is conquered, players from the conquering faction should help escort npcs from nearby friendly towns to the previously disputed area, and when they get there they begin rennovating it to look more the part of the side it now belongs to(ie, ne's take over an orcish town, npcs come and plant a new forest there while taking down the old orc setup). This will also make it impossible for any town behind the line of battle to be taken, as civilian npcs would not be able to cross the battle line.

Then, to balance the game and keep the winning side from gaining too much momentum, refuge npcs will spawn in the main city for the race that just lost territory and offer quests to win it back. That way players will have inncentive to win back their land.

On a further note, the longer an area is controlled by a particular faction, the less hostile the mobs in that area should be toward the faction. So in a horde controlled area, say, around a major city, monsters are less likely to argo on a horde race, while any alliance races in the area will have mobs chasing them down at every turn. That way players will want to try and take over as much land as possible, to ease questing.

Oh, and the best way to keep low lvl chars from having a hard time in enemy territory would be to make the npcs fighting on the line of battle too tough for anyone below mid-lvl(whatever that turns out to be) to get by. Then there don't have to be safe zones. Also, while all out, no holds barred pvp is great, it makes grouping with aoe dependent chars a chore, so perhaps even behind enemy lines all pvp should be faction based.

If the whole line of battle thing gets implemented, there should also be quests to get past the line, to deliver supplies to a group of rogue spies or something, a mission that isn't about mindlessly killing things or collecting parts to or for something, but about trying to sneak into enemy territory and deliver a package without drawing attention to the spies(ie, they will not appear if a member of the enemy faction is within a certain distance). Then they should be able to give you espionage missions that would help your faction back at the front.

Oh, and the whole "behind enemy lines" thing can even satisfy the die-hard hardcore wanting players, if chars are not allowed to make a bind point behind enemy lines(so they respawn in friendly territory). This way there is a more physical consequence to dying, you get booted back to safety and lose your foothold in enemy territory. So players in friendly territory will have a reason to kill opposing factions(getting them out of the way), and players in enemy territory will have a reason to be stealthy like and not make waves(not getting put out of the way).It would also help someone who gets stuck on the wrong side of the battle and is too low in lvl to make it back through the line safely. They just have to get killed.

Because I now notice my longwindedness and tendency to make each paragraph greater in size then the one before, along with the fact that blizzard is unlikely to even read read this post, much less use the ideas presented, thereby making it rather pointless, I will now stop.

Feel free to comment, shred, flame, rend, add to, take from, laugh at, think about, or do just about anything else with my excruciatingly lengthy post.

Hmmm...good point limblessjack. We all hate portal campers(especially if we played AC2). Maybe all bind points within a certain distance of the line of battle would be disabled, or something. Also, there should be ways around the line, where the terrain is unsuitable for large-scale warfare or something to that effect, but where mobs are more prevalent due to the lack of soldiers in the area, or more difficult passage due to some geographical feature (such as a narrow ravine with enemy snipers on the wall, or a gate puzzle that requires a ritual to open). That way you don't really put too much of a limit on where players can go in the world, because it wouldn't be too great to get stuck in your race's area of control and not be able to go anywhere because you don't want to fight other players. This way you can still take your party into enemy territory and just hope that there are enough of you to discourage aggression while you frolic through the fields or something.

I think the whole point of the line of battle should be that there are benefits to taking land and even more benefits for winning it back. That way you won't have one faction taking over everything, because as soon as they start to build momentum all of the races of the opposing faction will want to take back what was lost, due to quests and rewards for doing so. Otherwise races that will clearly have way too many players, such as the night elves, will just dominate their entire continent, and that would be no fun.

Bottom line is that the actually battle should be limited and balanced to the point that it is not the main focus of the game. It should just be a way for high level content to actually have a purpose beyond random killing of enemy players.

As for the risk of one faction completely taking over another faction's land, there are a few ways to get around this.

One could be, once a faction has invaded the opposing forces land to a certain extent there could be some organized event in which the losing faction, with the aid of several NPC's, participates in a large invasion to take back their lost lands (giving them a slight feel of victory after having lost for so long).

Or another way (which I think is better than my last suggestion) is to make it easier for a faction to take back lost land (land that is on their side of the original line but taken over by the opposing faction) than it is to actually invade land that belongs to the opposing faction. This could be done by having a much stronger NPC guard presence towards the center of their land mass, and it decreases in areas further out (towards the battle line). This would make it increasingly more difficult for a faction to invade the other faction's land because of a stronger NPC guard presence as they move further in, but easier to reclaim their own lands because the opposing forces will have a weakend NPC presence in those areas. Hopefully this would allow for a battle line that would be shifting back and forth.

I can see some problems with this battle line system though. One would be the large amount of calculations required to keep track of all of these NPCs.
Another problem would be the vast majority of the player population would flock towards the battle line which is where the action is taking place. This would leave the inland areas virtually deserted, with the exception of new players but they probably want to get to the line as soon as possible aswell. There would need to be something in these inland areas to keep players interested in staying there.
