164. Re: Answers to some of people WoW requests | 11/7/2003 1:08:27 AM GMTST
You all have such great ideas concerning the pvp, the quests and the combat system and whatnot.

We want this and we want that. pvp should be fun, and combat should be yada yada.

How about actually giving suggestions as to HOW they could implement it, instead of just saying WHAT it should be like?

If a level 10 warrior would be able to beat a level 17 warrior in pvp, how should that be possible then?
What's the levels for, if a person 7 levels lower can beat you anyway?
Better equipment? Yes... but that would result in item farming galore.
Special skills? Well yes, but they are both warriors, they have the same skills. So it's really just a question of who uses the skill first and at the right time, etc.? That's already in the game... and all other mmorpg's for that matter.
Random hits/evades/dodges/miss ? Already there.

You gotta understand that it's all cause and effect mate.

If say we make a pvp system dynamic, and not just automated click-and-observe. That would indeed make things interresting. Now the outcome of a duel would in very high regard be dependent on each players individual combat experience and mouse-to-eye coordination.
But, and there is always a but, try and estimate how many people will be scared off by this. People who are not hardcore gamers, who just want it easy and mellow, but still want to participate in the big faction events and duels. They will most likely scurry off and avoid duels combat, which will probably end in a lack of interrest in the game.
Also in this scenario lag would be a HUGE issue, making duels completely unfair unless both parties have equal latency.

What exactly are they supposed to do? Think of the possibilities or your ideas, and then think of the side-effects of that idea. Because there are always some sort of side-effect.

Just my two cents.
