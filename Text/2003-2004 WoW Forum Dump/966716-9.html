"Divided We Stand. United We Fall"

Level: 30-40
Suggested Players: 3 (Quest is only available to parties of 3 or less)
Average Time: 1-4 hours (Depending on methods used)
Difficulty: Medium-Hard
Availability: Alliance Only (Although a Horde version could be created)

Description:
This is an unprompted quest available twice a month during the RL summer season that can be accepted by talking to a troubled looking NPC who randomly spawns in one of the main Alliance bases.

The NPC tells you about a underground system of caverns that he owns rights to. The caverns connect two Alliance outposts and were once used to attract tourists until some Harpie pests moved in. He succeeded in exterminating them last summer by installing some music boxes throughout the caverns. The tunes from the boxes would lull the fiends to sleep. Trapping the sleeping Harpies and flushing them out proved simple. Now that it's summer again, the Queen Harpie has led her minions back to the shady caverns. Your mission is to cleanse the caverns of the Queen so that the others will return to their old habitat. Then the old man can get back into the tourism business. Sounds easy right? Wrong. There's a very big catch.

Upon entering the caverns, you will notice almost immediately that the path branches off into three separate corridors. Also, there are large pools of ammonia infested waters that slowly drain HP and small trails that lead into dead ends.

Now before you dismiss this quest, listen to the setup.

The three corridors branch off and meet back together just before the conflict with the queen. Think of each of the corridors as being broken into 6-10 long "stretches". All players will need to venture down a different corridor in order to simplify matters for their other party members. In other words, players will be traveling down separate corridors, but will all be on the same "stretch" of their respective corridor.

Middle Path: The middle path contains levers that drain sections of ammonia pools in all three corridors. (For example: Player 1 pulls the first lever. Now all three players witness the ammonia pools drain out of Stretch #1. They may proceed without losing steady amounts of HP.

Left Path: This path contains switches that illuminate lanterns along all three corridors. These lanterns guide the players through a section of their corridor so that they may avoid all dead ends. (For example: Player 1 and Player 3 see that their corridors branch again and they are not sure which way to go. Player 2 hits the first switch. Laterns are lit in Strecth #1 that allow players to find the quickest way through the caverns.)

Right Path This path contains wind-up music boxes that play music within a section of the caverns for 5 minutes. This music lulls the Harpies to sleep, so that players may walk by un-noticed. (For example: Player 1 and Player 2 see agressive Harpies up at the end of the turn. Player 3 winds up the first music box. All three players witness as the Harpies in Stretch #1 fall asleep.)

Notes:
Players continue this process of teamwork to make it through the caverns with the least amount of difficulty.

Players can not see each other as they move along their paths, however, they may send chat messages to communicate.

There are other creeps within the caverns that allow challenges for the players.

After 5 minutes of music, Harpies wake up. If you pass a stretch of a corridor and they wake up behind you, you'd have to run through them to wind-up the music box again.

The Queen Harpie should take about 10 minutes to beat with all three players working together.

A player may solo this quest, but doing so would probably involve a lot of back-tracking and may take 4-5 times longer.

A player venturing through the middle corridor is essential and would be impossible to complete otherwise.

Reward: Harpie Slayer Skill/Title and all the advantages that come with it.

Halt! Who are you and what business do you have in Durkeep!

Sneering, the guards haul you in to the town.

All outsiders must be taken to our leader, Toeschill the Mage. Hell know whether you are lying or not.

Very soon you stand in front of an imposing tower at the back of the town. Big leering gargoyle statues line the steps to the door, and as you pass, you swear you could see the gargoyles watching you. The door opens with a creak and the guards shove you in.

He is expecting you. Hes at the top.

With that the guards close the doors and the room is plunged into darkness. Wondering where the stairs were and how you are going to find them, light bursts into the room as torches lining the wall are lit up.

Finding the stairway, you climb and climb until you reach another set of doors. You open them to step into another room. The walls are lined richly with fabrics and a roaring fireplace gave the room an unsettling warmth. And in one corner is the Mage, a tall but frail old man.

Ah..so you are the guest from the outside. And you seek shelter within our walls? Very well, but you must do something for me. Outside this town lies a small abandoned house. It once belonged to my close friend, Cucu Byrd, a powerful mage. He died some time ago, but his books and work remain in the shack. Bring me 5 books and anything else you find and I will reward you handsomely.

Part B:

Objective Find the dilapidated house of Cucu Byrd and collect 5 books and anything else you uncover from his house. Be wary, anything could have moved in between the time of his death.

Objetive Update Defeat the Guardians
