324. Re: Just Curious | 2/1/2004 1:31:30 PM GMTST
Keeping things as brief as possible;

EVERQUEST
Like:

1. The vast variety of the environments and creatures in the game. Regardless of the theme
you enjoyed chances were good it was there. Exploration of new areas was fun when I played
(initial and Kunark expansion).

2. The stupidly incredible amount of items in game, and figuring out what each last one was
for, and they all pretty much had some use, be it crafting, quest, or combat.

3. The roles each character class had in the Eq world in the beginning were very well
defined.

Dislike:

1. The game was designed to demand more and more of your time as you progressed in levels
and was designed to be addictive. In the end you had to give more of your real life time to
Everquest to keep playing, or quit it. (I quit, but as we all know, too many tragic stories
abound the Net)

2. Everything in the game was more or less designed to be a time sink. Literally
everything, and it was obvious and utterly not fun.

3. The storylines in Eq quickly degenerated after the Kunark expansion. The game lost a
driving storyline, and without it, became simply "the grind".

4. The graphics quality is poor, and the artistic merit of the graphics is questionable.
The same goes for EQ2, although I see that the graphics are high technology, they still look
bad to me because they are bad artistically.

5. Bad music/sound effects

ASHERONS CALL 2
Like:

1. The game world was beautiful at full settings and again fun to explore.

2. I liked being able to pick the skills I learned and improved at level ups.

3. Ongoing storyline that was well integrated with the quests and overall game.

Dislike:

1. There were not enough players in the world, it was at first interesting, because running
into someone was an event, but later became tiresome as grouping became necessary.

2. Not enough variety in the environments and character races/classes.

3. Crafting system interesting but ultimately poorly balanced.

4. Big empty world.

DARK AGE OF CAMELOT
Like:

1. The classes and races were interesting and the quest system an improvement on that of Eq at the time.

2. Crafting was well done in respect to weapons and armor.

3. The ongoing warfare was between the three sides was great.

Dislike:

1. Overall, the game had a very medicore and average feel to everything.

2. While the crafting was well done, it did not have enough variety.

3. The three sides were never well balanced so warfare between the three was not always
fun.

SHADOWBANE
Like:

1. I loved the idea that you could build property and cities in game.

2. Character creation was more involved than most of the other games of it's type.

Dislike:

1. Poor graphics and poorly designed interface.

2. Lack of content.

3. Terrible amount of bugs and gameplay issues.

4. Game totally unbalanced.

FINAL FANTASY XI
Like:

1. The world is beautiful and the "feeling" of a Final Fantasy world comes across clearly.

2. The world is full of stories and the quests are really well done, especially instancing
boss fights.

3. The class system allowing you to combine 2 classes at once, and have your character try
all classes without restarting the character was fantastic. Furthermore, at least some of
the classes are atypical of the MMORPG mold.

4. The auction house works extremely well, it is automated, efficient, especially being
able to see previous sell prices for your items.

5. The "PlayOnline" mini-browser/e-mail client. I personally liked this little PDA type
program for organizing friends and such out of game. Could definitely use a chat function,
especially guild chat.

Dislike:

1. The extremely party-heavy game balance.

2. The interface, while learnable, is still terrible.

3. I could feel the grind coming with every level, and therefore it was time to quit. In
short, the time sink element of the game's experience point and skill systems intruded
exponentially in my fun as I progressed into the game.

I have played a quite a few MMORPG's, everquest, daoc, anarchy online, asherons call 1/2, etc, but I guess it would be best to just focus on the one I played the longest, everquest.

Examples;
Backstory: Unless you went to non-official websites there was not a lot of lore to be found out of game. In game however was a different story. So many quests, chatter, items and so on fed you bit by bit the history of the world you were in. It was nice to be dungeon crawling and stumble upon a item which you could tie to another living or dead NPC far accross the world..and you knew from the storyline you got from said npc how it got there.

Quests: As said before quests that are tied into the history in some way or fashion. Then again any quest even the silly ones are always nice. Another of the things I liked alot about everquest was that I could speak with random npcs and get quests..did not know what they were for, but did them anyway, often times ending up with items not even for my class...But it was fun anyway.

Junk items: Strange, but often times running across items that could be considered "vendor trash" help immerse you into the world, everquest had some of these...not as much as I would like, but some. Music: Ah yes, some of the music in everquest fit so perfectly with the zone/culture design(well, most of it). The erieness of paineel, the dankness of befallen, all of which is accented by good musical scores.

Travel: Yes! I loved waiting on boats belive it or not...Teleports, the books, and so on made the world seem so much smaller. Key thing about everquest for me..It had atmosphere, it had feeling, I could go on and on about the little things that tied it all together for me, but I belive bliz being gamers themselves knows what makes a good game world.

Overly obvious timesinks. I'm not talking about having to search for the items to create a new weapon with smithing. I mean, as it is in EQ, timesinks like their horrid tradeskill system that requires you to repeatedly combine excessive ammounts or items for a single skill up, wasting both time and $$. Timesinks of a degree are pretty much a necessity, but they shouldn't seem like time sinks. They should either be fun, or involving (pref a bit of both) but not repeatative and monotonous.

I like community/raid events that aren't too limiting that force people to be excluded (again, like LDoN raids in EQ with a stupid # cap). No one wants to be left out for any reason when there is no logical reason other than imposed game limitations.

I strongly believe in the KISS methodology. Keep It Simple Stupid. Fun isn't "farm Items A, B, C, and D as a rare drop off a rare creep, Turn into rare spawning NPC to trigger the questgiver for the 50 step quest to gain entry to the dungeon of You need a Life". If something needs to be that complex, then the steps should be mostly triggered events. And even then, the number of steps should be limited. I like the Idea of one quest leading into another, as it gives you a break and prevents things from getting overly complicated.

Finally, encounters need to be balanced with their difficulty/reward. Something EQ in all its years has yet to get a clue of. Items should not have limitations set so that by the time you can use it, its already outdated. This is why I strongly oppose level limits of any kind. If items need to be limited, limit them based on skill. And then, if it is limited, it should still be usable by someone who can normally use an item of that type, even if at a decreased effect (lowered armor/damage/stats/etc.)
