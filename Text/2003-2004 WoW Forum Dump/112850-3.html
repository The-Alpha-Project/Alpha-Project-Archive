45. Re: World of ZooCraft | 5/3/2003 4:30:40 AM GMTDT
Rocksurly, clearly you DO want this to be World of Tolkiencraft, and you fail to recognize that elves, dwarves, and orcs aren't especially original or imaginative, either for Blizzard OR Tolkien. As I said, all of these races are just human beings with very minor trait changes. Yes, I've read Tolkien, and yes, I'm familiar with much of his source material. Elves = Sidhe, and a romanticised view of the aristocratic nobility from an era that was vanishing when he wrote, but he longed for. Tolkien didn't like the modern world, and wrote of a idealized medieval one, before all this messy egaltarian politics and industry that gives us things like cheap food and houses. Dwarves really do exist in the real world, dwarfism is a disability, and they were commonly used as entertainment. Not exactly "original".

Blizzard has taken these standard races from the modern fantasy genre, and put their own twist on them. I love where they've gone with the Night Elves, the dwarves are typically Scottish, but their females are revolutionary: tough Eastern European women.

Blizzard has NO OBLIGATION to follow what other fantasy authors or game designers have done. They went in a different direction with their Orcs, moving from the brutish, ugly, and evil model descended from Tolkien, to noble barbarians with a shamanistic culture and deep sense of honor. The Tauren are a very original addition: aesthetically descended from minotaurs, but with many of the ethnic qualities of plains Indians like the Navaho and Sioux.

The Pandaren are a deliciously original creation; an Asian flavour to the game combining influences from China & Japan, and making great use of an iconic creature. Why be afraid of anthromorphization in a game that has ALWAYS taken a tongue-in-cheek approach, doesn't take itself too seriously, and clearly lets the artists try new things?
