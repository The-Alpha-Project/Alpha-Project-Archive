:: Introduction to the WoW Universe
The game is set four years after the end of Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos, in a ravaged Azeroth, wracked with tension. It is up to players to develop the skills and forge the alliances that allow them to survive in this time of strife. - Blizzard World of Warcraft is a MMORPG type game, which stands for Massively Multiplayer, Online Role Playing Game. Basically, tons of people playing together in one world and interacting with each other. WoW features a full 3D world that will include day/night passage, towns, dungeons, castles. This type of game however has a small drawback, Blizzard will have to charge a monthly fee to keep the servers updated. But don't get me wrong there, the quality will be much better than the Battle.Net we know of right now. MMORPGs usually offer an advanced help system online, with many GMs (Game Masters) online that will answer pages of stuck players or ones that are affected by a bug. Cheaters, harassers, etc , are also dealt with easily since the support is active, but also since they can get completely blocked off the server if they get cough.


:: Combat system
From what we know right now, I can tell that combats won't be action-filled like you might be used to in other RPGs. Blizzard has decided to make it a random-skill system for the battles. making it more balanced for the players with modem connections etc. That kind of system works in that way : your character will have a defense rating, attack rating, damage , etc, and with random rolls, they will be compared with your opponent's in order to decide who has it, and if you block it. There will be a life bar at the top much like Diablo showing the remaining health of the enemy, as well as its name. The battle can end if either one of the two opponents dies (lets hope here its not you :) or you will have the option to flee, but the monster will receive an extra blow and might start running after you.

Basically, combat will be quite simple if that method is used, but also more convenient if you need to use spells or items, because browsing a list of 100 items or spells might be hard if you have to interact with the combat (click everywhere etc)

::Death Management
Blizzard has been quite quiet on this matter, but they did tell us their wish not to make death stop you from doing heroic actions :
Q u o t e:
One of the things that we wanted to do was not to distance people from doing any cool heroic things like exploring the world to dying for their friends. We didn't want the penalties for death to be so sever that everybody has to play super conservatively. So of course we're going to have some penalties so players don't do things totally frivolously, but it shouldn't be so painful that it ruins your play experience for the entire night."


:: Skill System
Q u o t e:
"One of the things we wanted to achieve with the skill system in this game is to make it easily understandable and accessible and at the same time let you flavor your character and make independant choices so that your level 50 warrior would feel different from another level 50 warrior but not give you so much flexibility that you could screw yourself."

It seems like Blizzard chose a level / skill system much like Diablo 2, but they didn't go very deep yet in the details of how it'll work. There will be core skills, like magic for a mage, where even if you don't spend points you can't be terrible at using it, and that will be learned faster. Each class will also have a variety of secondary skills, from which each person will be able to customize his character differently, to some extent of course.

::Traveling
Have you ever wondered what will it be like to travel long distances in WoW ? If you played previous MMORPGs, you probably already know it can be really tedious, not to say boring, to travel from one place to another while walking or barely running. Blizzard even said that WoW's world would be one of the biggest in any MMORPG to date, so there has to be some way to ease this traveling! Past online games brought a few solutions, here are a few:

Transportation portal, or 'ringways (Asherons Call)
Scripted horses, i.e. you don't control it, it goes from point A to B (Camelot)
Player controlled mouts : You actually buy an animal you can ride (EQ, UO)
Teleportation NPC rituals (DAoC)...

:: Mounts
Each race has a unique mount (apart from Tauren which are mounts themselves), which they can control freely through the world. Through a Riding skill, players will be able to gain greater benefit from their mount, as well as gain the ability to use other race's animals or even wild ones. From what we have heard from Blizzard, only high level players will have access to the good mounts, while low-level player will have to rely on their own race's mounts or travel by other means (read below). The reputation with the race you wish to use a pet also plays a role, since you won't be able to buy one from an Orc if you don't have a good reputation with them (he won't sell you one). Once bought, mounts are summonable through a scroll in your inventory. They cannot die or be lost, and instantly appear / disappear when you use the scroll. They should also provide some bonus armour while riding, but such bonuses are still secretly discussed at Blizzard.

Most race's get their own mounts:
Humans - Horses
Orcs - Wolves
Tauren - None
Dwarf - Ram
Night Elf - Night Saber panther
Undead - Nightmares
Gnome - Unknown
Troll: Unkown

:: Transport
Unlike mounts, the other transportation means like Ships, Gryphons and Zeppelins cannot be freely controlled and must be set on course to a valid destination point that you previously 'flagged', or visited. As an example, a human player will go to a Gryphon nest in Dun Morogh, and ask to be traveled to Stormwind, which he was born in. The player will then start flying and will be able to look around the world while he travels. Boats and Zeppelins work the same, but they also add bring some mini-games to make the time pass faster. Ships will get mini-quests (fighting invaders etc) while Zeppelins will get some gambling games.

The known transports are:
Gryphon (for human, gnome, and dwarf)
Hippogryph (for NE)
Wyvern (for tauren, orc, and troll)
Some bat creature (for UD)
Zepplin
Boat

::Skills & Potions
In addition to those two traditional traveling ways, various magic scrolls and potions will be available to the player to enhance their speed, or grant them temporary immunity to travel in dangerous locations. Warrior class wouldn't get such spells, but would still be able to use a potion for example. Taurens also get their unique 'sprint' ability which will allow them to travel as fast as another race's mounted players.

:: Classes
Classes are what makes each race and character diffrent, and is also the core of character evolution in WoW. By choosing one, you get access to a variety of skills, some primary ones related to your class, and secondary trading skills. The class section will eventually offer strategy and guides to choosing your favorite class, but for the time being we only have what Blizzard wants us to have, which is only small information on the overview of each class. You may select a class from the list below for more information.

Druid:
Druids are a nature-base class with healing, summoning and shapeshifting abilities. When shifted, they can only use that form's abilities. These forms include bear, condor, and most interestly, rabbit. The rabbit form cannot attack, but also does not get attacked by normal monsters, making it a good scout. While in the rabbit form, you also appear the same "color" has neutral monsters, as not to attract suspicion.

They prosses the ability to tame beasts as their pet which will fight for them, and could also be used as mount. You will be able to tame your pet as well as have him gain level by fighting. They are similar to the hirelings in Diablo 2 expansion - they get various skills, and level as you do, but a little slower.

Mage:
From a popular idea, you'd think a mage is a weak character that does a tremendous amount of damage, but can't really survive a monster on his own. Blizzard wants to make this different, by making them tough enough to be able to solo when they can't, or don't want to join a group. Some of Diablo 2's spells are back to help this, such as Frozen Armor, which protects and freezes enemies, as well as Fire shield, which damages enemies over time, and of course a lot of offensive spells (Blizzard, Rain of Fire, etc).

Following this path might not be the easiest, but it sure will be one of the most rewarding, both by giving the player an incredible firepower, but also grant them with stunning spell visual effects. You can take a look at our extensive spell list to learn more about the mage's powers.

Since shamans call upon the spirits of the spirit world rather than energy like the mage, it often doesn't have much direct-damage spell-based attacks (still has a few ...), and could be classified as a hybrid warrior-mage, much like a druid would be, allthough 'shaman' refers to summoning nature where a druid is more likely to use nature's powers.

Warlock:
The warlock is a fire and shadow magic caster who can cast direct-damage spells, like Firebolt and Death Coil, as well as spells that cause damage over time, like Immolation and Corruption, which drain hit points continually. Most important, the warlock is an expert in demonic magic who, as he/she levels up, will accumulate a suite of demons-including (in increasing order of strength) imps, felhounds, succubi, and void walkers-that can be summoned as pets. Unlike other summoning classes, however, the warlock develops a specific relationship with his pets. The imp he summons will be the same imp with the same name every time (A demon that dies in combat will revert to a demonic plane and recharge over time.) Unlike mage pets, you can control demons directly-they'll have their own interface so you can use them to attack specific targets or use specific abilities. Finally, warlocks can create a powerful item called a Soul Stone that will instantly revive anyone who is carrying it upon death.

Introduction to character creation:
When you start the game, you will be able to choose from the 8 (Blizzard said there would be 8) available racse. There will also be a choice between male and female characters for all races. You will probably enjoy customizing them, as you can choose skin color, face model, hair stile and such. You will be able to see what you are building since there is a full-3D avatar that you see while you setup your attributes. As of now, there are no reports saying that you assign any stat points, so I guess Blizzard wanted it to remain simple. When you're done with that , the adventure begins, but you should take a look at this section so that you know exactly what character to choose :)

Affiliation:
Players in WoW will see themselves affiliated with a group of races, the Horde or the Alliance (might have more, but that's all we know more). Each alliance will provide their racial members with a better faction, and more compatible items. On the other hand, the opposite faction towns and items will be hostile to them, thus much harder to visit and cooperate with.

Possible Others:
It has been mentioned and even rumored that Blizzard will add more races. The final races known were just introduced in the October issue of Computer Gaming World ( Go get yourself a copy )

They live to serve nature and maintain the balance between the wild things of the land and the restless spirit of the elements. Despite their enormous size and brute strength, the remarkably peaceful Tauren cultivate a quiet, tribal society. However, when roused by conflict, Tauren are implacable enemies who will use every ounce of their strength to smash their enemies under hoof. Under the leadership of their ancient chief, Cairne Bloodhoof, the Tauren allied themselves with the Orcs during the invasion of the Burning Legion. The two races have remained steadfast allies ever since. Like the Orcs, the Tauren struggle to retain their sense of tradition and noble identity.

:: Secondary Skills

Secondary skills are similar to primary class skill in the way that they need to be trained in order to be efficient in a particular ability. However, the way to train these might differ from primary skill (usage instead of points). Secondary skills won't be as much class-specific as primary ones, but still will only be available for certain classes (herbalism for mages for example).

Skills are displayed through a list in the character sheet. Skills with red text are hardest to use (ingrediants rarer), but make skill raise faster when you are successful. Grey is easy (as a matter of fact you always succeed) but much much less skill gain.

The list below only contains a basic list of what we have heard, as well as short description of what it should do. Blizzard hasn't released more information on this yet.

Global Skills

Alchemy:
In combination with herbalism and other various skills, alchemy is an ability in creating various healing and stat enhancement potions. This is probably related with spell-user classes.

Cooking:
Not sure yet what you'll be able to cook, but it seems Blizzard is planning to have trade skills work with another one. Cooking could be used with fishing for example to make the fish you catch worth more life point when you 'use' them.

First Aid:
This will probably be used with health kits, bandages, and such items, and most probably available for Warrior classes.

Fishing:
Ability to use a fishing rod to catch various kind of fishes. We still don't know what you will be able to do with fish though.

Gathering:
By learning this skill you can extract ingredients from bushes herb, it will also shows dots on the minimap where herbs are.

Herbalism:
As mentionned before, this skill is used to create and transform various kind of herb for medicinal (first aid) usage or potion-making (alchemy). Using your "find herbs" ability, you can quickly pinpoint spots on your minimap that have useful plants.Specific types that give specific ingrediants only spawn in specific areas, like in caves, coasts, etc.

Language:
Language will be a secondary skill and it will require you to upgrade to speak to another race, for example a Human and Orc cannot understand each other until they learn the language.

Leatherworking:
Most probably the ability to craft leather-based armor and components.

Lockpicking:
Ability to pick various door and chest locks with the use of lockpicks.

Mining:
Seen on two screenshots published on May 13th, we can see that dwarves at least will have that ability. More to be known soon hopefully !

Riding:
The riding skill will allow a character to use a mount that isn't native to his race, as well as enhanced speed.

Tailoring:
This skill reflects how good a character is in making shirts, pants, as well as cloth armor. We are not sure if tailoring and leatherworking have anything in common yet.

Trade:
Bonus to merchant prices/costs. Higher level trade skills might require the player to go to Lab/Smith.

Wilderness Survival:
Various potion and item effeciveness will be determined by this skill.

:: Warcraft History

Here is a relatively short summary of what happened in the World of Warcraft since its creation in year -10000. This information is based on some official timeline released some time ago. Hopefully this will help you figure out what is going to happen next!

Creation of the World:

Year -10,000:
The Night Elves emerged in Kalimdor. Throughout their existence they have meddled with primitive magic, but not always with great success; in fact, through their attempts they accidentally let loose the demonic Burning Legion upon the world of Azeroth. The result is a massive war between the Night Elves and burning demons, in which the Night Elves are forced to form an alliance with the ancestral Dragons, which results in an apocalyptic conflict. Much of the continent of Kalimdor is devastated and sinks under the Great Sea. Alt hough the Burning Legion killed most of the Night Elves, the ones that survived vowed never again to use the ancient magic.

Year -6,000:
Unwilling to live without magic, a rogue band of Night Elves is banished from Kalimdor. They travel to the newly formed Eastern lands where they find the kingdom of Quel'Thalas and developed a new society we now refer to as High Elves. They have a very hard time avoiding skirmishes and raids by the local Troll tribes.

Year -2,900:
Several thousand years pass, the Human Empire if Arathor is developing in Lordaeron.

Year -2,700:
The High Elves are on the verge of losing everything to the Trolls. In a last fight for survival, they agreed to ally with the Humans and to teach them magic in exchange for help against the Trolls. The combined armies of Humans and Elves succeeded in sweeping out most of the Trolls.

Year -2,600:
However, a price was to be paid. The Humans showed little respect for the magic they have learned, and once again Burning Legion's armies are attracted to the World by the magical forces. The Order of Tirisfal is founded several years later. They become the guardians of the world against the Legion, although their Order is shrouded in secrecy and the general population knows nothing of them.

Year -1,200:
Inflicted by several conflicts, The Human empire of Arathor is divised into seven individual nations: Azeroth, Lordaeron, Stormgarde, Kul Tiras, Gilneas, Dalaran and Alterac.

Year -45:
Guardian Aegwynn defeats Sargeras, the Burning Legion's Lord, in a one-on-one combat. The tormented soul of Sargeras ties itself to Aegwynn's future child.

Year -40:
Although they are eventually banished from Azeroth by the Order of Tirisfal, the Burning Legion corrupts the Orcish clans on the world of Draenor. A once noble, shamanistic Orc society is quickly transformed into a vicious Horde, ruled by the malevolent Shadow Council. These powerful learn the basics of Dark Magic from the demonic invaders, such as Gul'dan who is possessed by Kil'jaeden (Sargeras' apprentice)

Year -37:
Aegwynn gives birth to his child, Medivh, who's destined to becoming the last of the Gardians. However, she gives him all of her powers and knowledge and seals it deep inside him so that he would only use it when he becomes an adult.

Year 1:
Medivh, fouled by Saergeras' soul, contacts Gul'dan in a thirst of conquest. He opens the Dark portals, and the Horde begins invading Azeroth.

Warcraft: Orc and Humans:

Year 5:
Medivh is killed by his best friend, Lord Lothar, and his apprentice Khadgar. During the last 4 years, the Horde was successful in conquering Azeroth and Khaz Modan, and established themselves in Blackroc Spire as their primary base.

Lord Lothar leads a group of refugees to the northern lands of Lordaeron, where he is successful in reuniting the 7 Human nations to fight the Horde. This new alliance will be remembered as the Alliance of Lordaeron.

Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness:

Year 6:
Orgrim Doomhammer, the Horde's Warchief, launches his huge fleet to invade the coasts of Lordaeron by see. They invade the land up to its core and destroy Quel'Thalas, kingdom of the High Elves.

The Alliance succeeds in pushing them back, but Lothar is killed during the siege of Blackrock Spire. The Human armies bring Blackrock Spire to dust and push back the Horde to the vicinity of the Dark Portal.

The Dark Portal is destroyed and the Horde is no more.

Year 7:
Remaining Orcs are captured and jailed in the Alliance's keeps.

Warcraft II: Beyond the Dark Portal:

Year 8:
Ner'zhul, an Orcish shaman, brings together the remaining clans in Draenor and opens the Dark Portal. He orders his armies to steal many of Azeroth's artifacts in order to open multiple portals in Draenor.

The Alliance sends his men, lead by the sorcered Khadgar, beyond the Dark Portal to stop Ner'zhul's plans. The two armies fight in the infernal world of Draenor for months.

Strengthened by the stolen artifacts, Ner'zhul opens many more Portals in Draenor in a hope to invade the Human world. However, before he can send his troops through, he losses control over their energy which begins sweeping away Draenor's life force. The heroes of the Alliance, knowing they would be imprisoned there forever, are forced to destroy the Dark Portal to save Azeroth from the infernal energy.

Draenor is devastated.

Year 9:
The Alliance once again heals and the Orcish are once again jailed in keeps. Only a few Orc clans can escape and they hide in wild plains.

Thrall's Tale : Lord of the Clans:

Year 22:
Thrall, a young Orc raised as a slave and human gladiator, escapes and begins his quest to discover his lost ancestral history. Thrall reunites a small group of clans and leads them to stand up against the guardians of the Alliance.

Year 23:
With the growing threat of a new Horde, once again the Alliance is struct by internal conflicts between the leaders.

Thrall becomes the new Warchief of the Horde and introduces back the lost shaman culture to his troops. The Horde, renewed with this new strength, looses the last scars of the corruption that were caused by the Burning Legion.

A civil war between the Human nations threatens their very existence at the very moment the Burning Legion returns to their world

Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos:

Year 39:
A hard earned peace is established since the war ended. Hoever, while the Human kingdoms are still blinded by their past victories, the Orcish clans slowly unite to a young promising chief.
A shadow even darker is born, threatening to remove all life from the World. A war begins once again, as the inevitable hour of Chaos draws near. When the sky rains fire, the world will tremble to the might of the Burning Legion.

World of Warcraft:

Year 45:
Four years passed since the end of Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos. The conflicts that once nearly destroyed the world of Azeroth are no more. Every race, weakened by war, begin the building of a new Kingdom. However, an ancient and forgotten power is about to be brought to the surface once more...
