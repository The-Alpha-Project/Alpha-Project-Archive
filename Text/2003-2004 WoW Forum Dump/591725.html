7. Re: a PVP system (long read) | 9/24/2003 4:01:37 AM GMTDT
Your point system sounds like it would well as a faction system for orcs/humans etc... I would also include a confirmation window so if you die to a casters aoe, you dont have to give them the penalty if you dont believe it was intentional.

My ideal server would be a ffa no level limit server designed for pvp with factions (no, I dont think every server should be like this).

Create faction based penalities for healing, buffing, or grouping with members of opposing factions and killing members of your own faction. These penalties are less severe if the person on your team that you kill has a lower. It would even be positive for killing someone with negative faction just like killing a traitor. These faction ratings would change the difficulty of getting quests, training through the vendors of your hometown, and whether or not guards will kill you on sight.

You would always be apprehensive to the other teams even if you try to play a human on orc faction or something. You already betrayed your human kin, why wouldn't you easily betray the orcs?

Points could be gained through killing enemy factions, completing quests, and pooling faction points with guildmembers.

Rules would have to be in place to avoid the farming of faction points. Decay over time of negative faction would lead to leaving your character idle to work off kills like in UO, but you could still play your character. A guild based pooling system that would gradually adjust the factions of all guildmembers towards the mean would also allow individuals to identify the faction of another character quickly. Guilds would want everyone to be on the same faction for the highest level quests, protection from guards, and the best merchant prices.

Guards in towns should get progressively tougher as you get to the center, but you should be able to organize sieges of other cities with economic benefits for holding an enemy city. Enemy guards and players would continue to respawn, so holding the city for a prolonged period of time would be quite difficult. Factionless cities would also exist without guards that constantly respawn unless more are purchased. A voting system would be in place to elect city officials from the player base who could set the tax rate, purchase city defenses, and broadcast messages. You could never destroy an enemy stronghold, but you could hold it for periods of time with the right effort. Factionless cities could be held indefinitely with no effort if noone else tries to take it from you. This might prevent the game over aspect of shadowbane where it is too costly to rebuild a city after a 3am raid causing people to quit.

GM intervention on the server would only be for events, bugs, and technical problems. Players can swear, harass, or do whatever they want and police themselves with who they associate,the chat filter, and the ignore command. GM's can just put people that want to swear at them on ignore rather than ban.
