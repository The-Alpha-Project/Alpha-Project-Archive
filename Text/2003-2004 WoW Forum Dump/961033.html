Well, I can only speak for myself, here, so here goes:

I witnessed the game running when I was at GenCon.

I have not seen another game out that does what WoW does-- it made me feel as though I were actually THERE-- in the World of Warcraft.

No screenshot does justice to the game when it is in motion-- movements of grass/plants, little animals and birds, the lapping water of rivers and lakes, the roar of waterfalls, the sound effects of your character's footsteps and weapons, musical score playing... You have to experience the game in its totality to believe it.

I also was fortunate enough to watch a dev demo Shadowfang Keep's instanced dungeon encounters, and I can say that the graphics alone again blew me away, far exceeding my expectations. Add in the fun factor-- the idea that the dungeon had its own traps and quests and NPCs to interact with (besides just killing monsters mindlessly) and you basically have a game-within-a-game right there-- with its own backstory and all. And this is just a small part of the WoW experience.

This is what Blizzard has been trying to accomplish, and in my opinion, they have done it, and I was watching an alpha version. When retail hits, it will blow everyone away.

Now, if you PREFER the more "realistic" graphics of EQ2 or whatever, then by all means, go play that game. I would just hate to see you pass up WoW without giving it a chance. If you do, it's your loss.
