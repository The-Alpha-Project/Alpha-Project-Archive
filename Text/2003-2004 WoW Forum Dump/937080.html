113. Re: A Novel Idea. | 1/14/2004 6:07:41 AM GMTST
For video game junky, I would consider myself 'getting up there' in age. I too am a professional with responsibilities. It seems to me that a complaint that many people have is that they have personal expectations for the game and they find that those are crushed, not necessarily by the game itself, buy by other players. They quit playing because they can't see their way to pay a fee for something that no longer holds enough pleasure for them - but could - if the actions of the population were different.

In a perfect world, a MMORPG server would exist that excluded all those actions or people that annoy you. Since it is impossible for everyone to have their own server (not to mention silly as you would be very lonely) we have to shoot for groups of people. Personally, I like the idea of a casual sever, as I like the idea of a role-play sever. Both severs, in theory, exclude acts that I, and many other older gamers, find take away from the gaming experience. I would venture a guess that many folks find themselves with similar tastes. Others like the idea of a full PvP server, or a fully cooperative server. Everyone has their own expectations.

Those things being said, it is nigh time that a game developer carved out a niche for the old timers among us (and the old at heart) who grew up on pen and paper rpgs, Bard's Tale on our C64s, and glorious text MUDs. Oh, some games have professed to have 'role-playing servers' that originally appealed to many of us, only to later be let down by the lack of adherence to the rules by the population due to the lack of rule enforcement. I suggest that Blizzard create a server type that caters to the older population. Its features would consist of the following:

1. RP name restricted - actually enforced. Immediate name change for all those who break the policy. Immediate deletion for those who choose inflammatory or offensive names. What does this accomplish? It gets rid of the immersion shattering Shadow3, MrBigglesworth, and Gandalff.

2. Time restricted advancement. Real time constraints on level land marks. For example: Takes 30 real days minimum to reach level 15, 40 real days to reach level 20, etc. What does this accomplish? It cures the power-leveler vs. casual gamer problem with out restricting play time.

3. Harassment policy: Most games have decent harassment polices in my opinion, however, they should step up their application of the consequences (1st warning, 2nd suspension, 3rd deletion). What does this accomplish? Real consequences for acts that purposefully detract from other player's experiences - limits griefing.

4. OOC remarks limited to personal tells or chat channels. Any public expression of OOC remarks will result in progressively punitive action - with actual follow through. What does this accomplish? Gets rid of l33t speak, overused immersion breaking acronyms and most spamming.

5. Increased monthly charge of $5.00, in addition to normal monthly fees, in order to use the server. What does this accomplish? Pays for the extra policing necessary to run the server in the above manner.

Now they could call this a role playing server, a casual server, a wussy ol' farts hangout, whatever. The point is, I would be willing to bet that a server with the above restrictions and features would thrive - though I could be wrong. Now, all we need is a MMORGP that has the nerve to role the dice and make the biggest effort to date to serve many of their customers in a manner that would most certainly inspire customer loyalty.



Zalya
