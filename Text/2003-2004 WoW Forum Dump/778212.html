1. Player Shop Poll | 11/23/2003 11:48:38 PM GMTST
What do people think about player controlled shops?

The idea is if you want you can take the stuff you make with your trade skills, and the stuff you find while questing, and instead of selling it all to a NPC vendor you can put it in a shop and put a price on it and see if other players come along and buy it.

Even if you are off-line your stuff will still be for sale in the shop. If it gets sold you get the sale price (less a commission for the shop vendor). If it doesn't get sold the stuff is returned to you.

Player shops exist in other MMORPGs and there are different ways to implement the idea. As far as I know Bliz hasn't told us yet how player shops will work in WoW (if there will be any at all).

Here are four possibilities:

1. No player controlled shops. Either sell all your stuff to NPC vendors or give it to friends.

2. Individual player shops. "I want my own private shop to put my stuff in!" Has the potential to clutter up the landscape (or city areas where the shops are located) with buildings.

3. Guild controlled shops. Have a stall in front of your guild headquarters with a shopkeeper selling stuff. Guild members will post stuff for sale. Guild will take a commission on sales, thereby generating guild revenue.

4. Central shopping bazzar or market in major cities and towns. All players post goods for sale in this market. Players will have access to many menues full of items for sale. City will take a cut of sales.

Vote for your preference. I personally vote for 3. :D
