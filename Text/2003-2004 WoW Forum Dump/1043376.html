"Watchoo Talkin' 'bout??"

Time: 5 minutes

Reward: This quest results in next to no reward, but it does unlock further quests with the wizard, including other translation quests that require you to translate from Tauren, Gnome, Dwarven, Orc, Common, Elvish, Trollish, and eventually others (Draconic, Furbolg, etc.). These could either be done by the player with the languages skill or by another player or an NPC.

Tower Troubles
Level: At Least Beginner Engineering Skill
Restrictions: Must be Engineer- Solo

As you walk along throwing your uncommenly large wrench in the air and singing the tune to "whistle while you work," you see a wonderful little (Gnomish/Goblin) town sitting in a lush valley. As the little people toil endlessly, gathering huge piles of stone and lumber for reasons unforseen, you walk up to the largest (Gnome/Goblin) around. This giant stands an astounding 4' 2" and carries a mace with a head the size of you fist (he's a little guy). Being an inquisitive engineer, you inquire about the buildup of such wonderful and quality goods.
"We just work ceaselessly for no good reason, would you like to- Oh No!"
You look in the direction of the chiefs stare and see an incoming wave of (insert standard creep).
"Those ____ will destroy our wonderful little town! If only there was some way to turn this pile of raw materials and these odd plans, which my family has been holding for many generations, of some sort of attacking building into a defense maybe we could save ourselves."
Your engineers intuition kicks in: " I have the skill and the will. I can save your town if you only let me see those plans of yours kind chief."
"Anything to save my town, please, save us! My workers will keep bringing resources to the pass in order to provide you with the necessary building materials. Hurry!"

Description: With all skills aside from you engineering skill deactivated (wouldn't want some mage to just cast some AoE spells and beat the quest) you must craft a cunning defense in the valley. Using the plans currently in your knowledge as well as the tower plans you were just given and all your cunning, save the town! The only rsources you can use will be coming in a steady flow form the workers as they carry them over to you from the stockpiles. You are able to build any of the gadgets you hav plans for (golems, mines or a giant trojan horse) but it is suggested to incorporate building some towers. 5 waves of increasingly stronger creeps will rush in the canyon towards the town. The chief can kill the first 5 let in, but any after that will quickly kill all of the townsfolk.

Reward: Upon killing the final wave of creatures, the chief gives allows you to keep the plans for his tower. The tower is a sort of extra strong pet that is stationary. It is not super good and ultra camping uber, but relatively inexpensive and worth using when there are large numbers of enemy or one very strong enemy around.

200. Re: List your quest ideas for Bliz. | 1/25/2004 12:45:36 AM GMTST
"An Old Friend"

Level: hard (several characters, all level 12+)

Time: long (several hours/possibly days)

An Epic quest for all races.

Introduction: As you enter a town, you notice the townsfolk seem depressed. The mood seems to permeate the very air you breathe. As your party passes through town, one of the townspeople comes up to you and says the town needs help and you look like hearty adventurers. He explains his name is Cain and the town has been besieged by a demon. The demon is holding the local priests son hostage and forcing the priest to do all sorts of dark arts. Cain is the town elder and he and the priest are old friends, so he is asking for your help to find the priests son so the priest no longer has to do the demons bidding.

Part 2: After adventuring in a nearby dungeon you find the priests son dead. Upon returning to Cain with the body he asks you to stop the priest, Lazarus. As a result of practicing these dark arts he has been turned mad. He has slain all his fellow priests and is attempting to summon more demons to terrorize the land. You must venture to the local temple, down into the abandoned monastery beneath the temple, find and then defeat Lazarus and any minions he may have already summoned.


Part 3: (Optional) As he lies dying after the killing blow, Lazarus whispers to you in a final moment of lucidity: he asks that you avenge him and his son by killing the demon Diablo. He gives you a stone that will close the portal forever, preventing any more demons from coming through, but asks that before you close the portal you go through it, find and kill Diablo. Suddenly Cain appears behind you (how did he get there?) and explains that Diablo isnt actually part of this world, but that he is a particularly powerful demon that has learned to manipulate the minds of people in this world from his own domain. He goes on to explain that confronting Diablo will be very dangerous. Not only is Diablo himself very powerful, but his domain is full of other powerful demons as well. He says that if you agree to go through, he has the power to prevent any further demons from coming through the portal because Lazarus is dead and is no longer feeding the portal his dark magicks.

Note: if the players refuse the last part of the quest and close the portal without defeating diablo, this opens up the possibility that a similar event might happen some time in the future at a new location.


Reward: Upon successful completion of each part of the quest, Cain gives you some sort of reward, can be many things. Gold, items, new skill, etc. If you agree to seek out Diablo, Cain gives each player an item designed to protect from demons. I.E. it weakens nearby demons/makes demons move and attack slower/makes the player harder for demons to attack/makes their attacks stronger vs demons, etc. This item wouldnt do all these things, but it might have 1 or 2 effects.
