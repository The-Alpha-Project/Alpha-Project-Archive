28. Re: Epic Boats, Buildings *** Warning idea *** | 7/20/2003 7:17:04 AM GMTDT
I fully agree with Forestknight. In my experience with MMORPGs, the novelty of a house - or any thing of a PC owned nature - wore off rather swiftly. Forestknight's idea is a great step in the right direction to making PC owned establishments USEFUL(<--important) and 'fun'.

Now, while I fully support his idea, I realize that many would resist this change to the accepted playing style of many games[If you have the $$$, you can own anything].

#1)One compromise would be that a single player could only own a puny little dinghy, and nothing immense like a Galleon(sp?). It only really takes one person to operate a dinghy. I can still see millions of those floating around in the harbor though...cluttering.....

#2)Another compromise would be to implement the maintenence fee. Perhaps the resources could be player made items: sails, mast...stuff like that. By extension, the craftsman who MAKE those items would need other resources - lumber, blahblabhh to make the items.

#3)Partially simplified #2, make the maintenence fee the lumber, etc. [Someone suggested this before] Make this 'fee' generally above what your average player would get.

Now, rather then limiting the pooling of resources to 'Guilds' - simply allow a group of people to form say...a 'business relationship' that allows them to pool their resources to have joint ownership of whatever the item is.

It lets the hardcore soloer not suffer the "indignity"[<--note sarcasm] of joining a guild, while allowing them to retain the ability to own large craft.


Of course, the third idea would require some enforcement measures, but it gives the 'solo'er some more, 'percieved' freedom.
