This one is from an old DOS game, Stunts, aka Stunt Driving. Basically, you can create your own tracks using tools on a set environment, then you can race them for times or against an computer AI. You had a choice of vehicles from a Hummer to a Porsche March Indy (the best in the game, funny, I didnt know KARTs could survive jumps). If you get hung up or collide at a certain speed, you usually die.

I designed a track that basically gave you almost the WHOLE width of the field to accelerate in. At the end was a little trap. Most of the track on this straightaway was elevated, about the third square from the edge it drops to normal track heighth. There I put a "Y" (next to last block on the field in that direction), to the right is where the track is supposed to go, straight led into a hill that ran smack into the border of the field. I had designed this track for JUST such an occassion. I picked the fastest car, put it up to max accelleration and speed and ignored the turn at that spot. My car MISSED the next to last square due to the speed I left the elevated road. It hit the hill, and ran up it at full speed. Ever notice in some games if your character gets caught on a border, you get reflected back at multiple times the speed? Well, that happened here as well as putting me straight up in the air. I got launched so high, I could barely make out the field (the track was lost about 15 seconds before I reached that point). Talk about your hang time, We gauged it to be about 3 minutes before I hit the track floor after I collided with the border. Needless to say, the car was a pixelated, burning mess.
