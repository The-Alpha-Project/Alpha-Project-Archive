Q u o t e:
So when you, Vanevar, die, in WoW... delete your gear.


I feel that item loss is too harsh a penalty and causes frustration among players.

However, if you really wanted to implement it in-game, I am sure there is a better way than having players take it upon themselves to delete their own equipment after a death. The best solution would probably be a hardcore server with special rules.

You see, the three reqirements for a good deterrent to any behaviour are as follows:

CERTAINTY
It must be certain that a negative experience or sanction will follow the behaviour.

CELERITY
Learning to avoid a certain behaviour is also correlated directly to how quickly after said behaviour you wish to deter occurs the negative experience or sanction occurs.

SEVERITY
Learning to avoid a certain behaviour is also correlated directly to how severe the negative experience or sanction is.

So based on this proven psychological and criminological theory, (And it IS proven, if you've taken any criminology or psychology courses in university you'd know this) let us critically analyze the death penalty in World of Warcraft.

1. Certainty:
Is there any certainty to the death penalty? If you die, is it certain you will become a ghost? Nope, someone could resurrect you. OK so if no one resurrects you, you appear at the closest bind, which is very certain, so next... Is it certain that a sanction will occur? Yes, it is certain, either you have to run back to your corpse, or, you will take a small exp loss. It is not certain, however, that you will recieve exp loss, and it is not certain that you will recieve time loss. That choice is left to the gamer, and it is very likely that the gamer will always choose the lesser penalty depending on the situation. Thus I can only give certainty a near perfect score.

Certainty: 9/10

2. Celerity:
Is there any celerity to the death penalty? If you die, how soon do you face the sanctions for death? The penalty for death is either a loss of time running back to your corpse, or taking a small exp loss. If you choose to take the loss of time, that is applied right away. If you choose to take the exp loss, that is applied right away. However, negative exp is the exception. You may die several times and rack up quite a large sanction against yourself, but log off for the rest of the day and not feel the sting of loss until the next day when you are questing and killing monsters and you realize "oh yea, I died a lot yesterday..." As the sting of loss is not always felt right away I can not give the celerity for WoW a perfect score.

Celerity: 9/10

3. Severity:
Is there any regularity to the severity of the death penalty? If you die just outside of a town, there will be hardly any severity to your sanction at all, it would take a short amount of time to walk to your corpse and rez yourself. If you are very far out in the wilderness, there will be a much more severe sanction, a long amount of time to walk to your corpse and rez yourself. In this respect, the severity of the death penalty will vary wildly depending on your current location and how long it takes to get back to where you were. However, assuming that the distance areas are from towns are directly correlated to their difficulty and reward (the areas for higher level characters will be far from towns), this makes perfect sense. If you attempt a high level quest the risks associated with dying also increase. That is, risk vs reward. Areas with higher reward *should* also have a higher risk associated with them.

The other type of sanction is exp loss. At this time it is completely unknown how much exp will be lost, although we are sure it will not be so much that it makes the game not fun (frustrating). The amount of this sanction will be weighed out during the beta I assume, but in the meantime, I'm going to give the severity of WoW a near perfect score. It is *just* harsh enough to make players respect the game world and deter death, but not so harsh that it frustrates players.

Severity: 9/10

Death Penalty Score: 27/30 - 90%

OK now lets explore my death penalty idea. Certainty: Currency loss is certain, based on your level. The player has no choice in the matter. 10/10

Celerity: Currency loss applies right away, but you may not feel the sting of loss until later, just like Blizzard's current model. 9/10

Severity: Currency loss is scaled according to your level, making the severity of death constant. It doesn't matter whether you died 5 feet from town or miles away from town, the penalty is calculated based on your level, meaning the severity increases with you as you level up, making the severity of death for higher level players more than for lower level players. Higher level players will go into higher level areas, with higher level creeps, and their chance of dying also increases, meaning that risk vs reward is balanced. If people find that currency loss at a certain level is too harsh, they can always balance that out in the same way they would balance out the amount of exp to be lost, during the beta. 9/10

28/30, 93%

These seem to be very comparable in their effectiveness as a death penalty. I realize though that if currency loss were the only sanction for death, rich players would find this as an acceptable way to death-travel and so, instead of replacing exp loss with currency loss, I will suggest that, like Diablo 2, it should be an additional penalty.
