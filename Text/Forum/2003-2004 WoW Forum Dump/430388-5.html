82. Re: about K I L L STEALING - interesting id | 10/15/2003 3:44:19 PM GMTDT
From the perspective of an AC player (whose gaming world is meant to be somewhat similar in proportions by a comparison of a leading Computer Games magazine to WoW), Kill Stealling mostly happens in one of two places. Neither of which is the open expanses of the world which are not instanced in WoW. You can travel a ways from town and very rarely bump into someone and if you do, unless they are deliberately trailing you then moving on with your hunt in another direciton soon leads back to solitude.

In the example cited at the beginning where two 'accidently' happen upon the same kill usually (consider it MMORPG courtesy) the player who arrived second to the area will move on, often even if they are attacked they will move on and leave the original occupant to their business.

The main places KSing becomes an issue are dungeons where people gather for either Experience or Loot. Having a finite resource (per spawn) of Mobs to kill can result in competition to finish them off, espseically during peak times. It is my understanding that the best Experience and Loot dungeons will be instanced, thus eliminating this issue quite effectively.

On the eventuality someone is stalking you in game for their own enjoyment, you can always move on and try to lose them, or even report the abuse/log. Its a game, the frustrations you might express will rarely do more than encourage the protagonist.

While further discussion should probably wait until we have experienced the game firsthand for some time, I at least feel comfortable with the expectations of KSing being far less of a concern than in other games.
