592. Re: Death Penalty Discussion | 4/14/2004 4:10:41 AM GMTDT
I hope and prey they actually check the Suggestions area of the Website, because I've been trying to keep a Death Penalty suggestion afloat for a few days now.

http://www.battle.net/forums/wow/thread.aspx?FN=wow-suggestions&T=27628&P=1&ReplyCount=11#post27628

I will now re-post it here.

The "ghost" form is supposed to have some unique features, like ghostly NPCs, and even ghostly quests. Thus, you can't really remove it.

But people are complaining that as a ghost, dying near a bindstone is no punishment. Also, when you die far away, running back to your body is dull.

With death-a-port, it's a free ride to town. It was a very easily abused system.

I have another idea: let's say that being a Ghost is a "time out". Once you die, you respawn as a ghost in a nearby graveyard. You get a few ghostly abilities, you can interact with other ghosts, have access to a Quest log, etc. However, you can't get back to your body, do tradeskills, or really anything. There may be a FEW things for a ghost to do, but they would be rare.

After a certain time limit passes, you begin feeling "drawn" to your body. At this point, you can either choose to release, and come back to your body (not running, simply teleported to it), or you can talk to the spirit healer, and be revived in the graveyard. This way, it's a punishment, but not a punishment where you spend time doing a mindless task (like running). Basically, you just float there, thinking about your mistakes, and maybe chat with the other Graveyard citizens. Of course, you can always venture out as a Ghost, and look for things, but being a ghost, you're unlikely to find anything interesting.

It may sound boring doing nothing for a set amount of time, but it would be a punishment. Also, doing NOTHING but talking to people often feels less tedious then running in a single direction.

Besides, like this, distance does not matter. If you die, no matter where you die, you are punished for it.

Something does need to be added so as you don't end up dying the moment you resurect...

(Here is also a response to people who said this wouldn't be a punishment to those who died in a dungeon)

According to all the Beta posters who spoke on this issue, as a Ghost right now, the longest corpse runs in the world are about five minutes. These are the ones where you ARE in fact deep in a dungeon.

Also, as a Ghost, getting back to where you were is nothing insanely difficult.

If a group is wiped out, it is no great difficulty to get back to their bodies.

What's more, the current corpse run even gives you the exact location of your body. It wouldn't take more time, or be difficult in any way. As of right now, people complain that it's tedious, and are demanding a ligher death penalty.

Running to a Corpse is no more difficult then holding down one button. Some even do it INSTEAD of letting their allies bring them back, because it takes little time (and Res sickness lasts too long). It doesn't stop those people, as they are running back, from thinking: "Man, is this boring."

Of course, it would still be possible that people would still do that, but the wait time could be adjusted. Also, the state your body returns in can also be adjusted. Not necessarily at Full Health, for example. Though, as a "bonus", the monsters could avoid you or not attack you for a short period of time. Rez sickness is a bad idea, since you already waited for such a long time.

As for the Spirit Healer... The exp penalty hardly seems like an option. People don't like to pick it. Perhaps the Spirit Healer could charge you money in proportion to the level; but money also seems hard to come by right now.

Maybe the "time out" as a ghost should be mandatory; however, once it runs out, you can choose to release back to the body, or talk to the Spirit Healer, and he will bring you back to life at the Graveyard, at full health, but with res sickness. Thus, it's still a "death-a-port", if you really just want to get OUT of the place where you were (you wondered deep into a high level monster nest, and you will die about three times on your way out), but it takes up a good deal of time from your next adventure.

This way, a group that's wiped out does not pop back up at full strength, but after a bit of healing and a light meal, can continue. However, a group that bit off way more then it can chew and stands no chance of getting out of where they died "alive" can return to town (with "time" the only cost), and not die twenty times as they run for the exit.

Also, as a side-effect, the graveyard would be full of Ghosts. We were promised that some classes, like Shaman, would be able to see, or even talk to the ghosts of players. However, as of now, there is no way to actually find non-npc ghosts. They aren't hanging out in the graveyard, they're madly dashing to their corpse. If they were on a "time limit", they would be able to do almost nothing BUT talk. They would stay in that same place, and mingle with the other dead. Graveyards could become rather bizzare social gatherings. As it is now, Graveyards would be places where ghosts appear, and start madly running away from. With this, they would actually become "haunted".
