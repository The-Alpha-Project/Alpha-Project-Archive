23. Re: Q&A 01/07/04 | 1/7/2004 5:54:43 PM GMTST
Q u o t e:
Can players customize the height & weight of their characters?

Height/Weight is unlikely to be changeable due to how it affects character animations and items on the character. Also if you change height and weight then the distinction between an orc and a human becomes much less when they are both wearing similar armor. The combination of wanting distinct animations and not wanting the animations to look wonky because they were being used on a model they were not quite meant for, made us decide to not allow changing of height/weight at this time.


Isn't that arguement rather silly? How come every other company can manage to add the variety of height and build/weight but not Blizzard. Is it really that much a challenge to the team? It's not like characters and weapons have realistic proportions anyway.

As for races looking too much alike, that could easily be fixed by limiting height and build by racial restrictions. Even the tallest dwarf won't be as tall as a human for example. Nor would the shortest dwarf be confused with a gnome.

I -really- hope Blizzard changes their mind on this. This has to be the silliest reasons to eliminate as basic things like height and weight for character generation. This did however add one thing to WoW's uniqueness. For the worse.

I have to agree with Kheldon here. I'm slightly sad that Blizzard has decided to omit a feature from World of Warcraft which will likely be a standard for character customization in MMORPGS to come. But if I may prod a bit let me ask why Blizzard has even decided to avoid a system as simple as that used in Dark Age of Camelot, a scaled system where models never change shape, only size. This system allows for size differences while having absolutely no impact whatsoever on character animations.

In DaoC each charcter has a size setting of small, medium, or large for their race. In addition, as Kheldon already suggested the tallest dwarf is still nowhere close to the shortest human. As a result the changes are subtle yet still sufficient to add more depth in the character generation process.

I realize its not an issue of top priority for the team. I simply hope its something they'll reconsider.

The arguement is not weak. Once you change the mesh of a model other than just scale animation can become screwy. I know... Its happened to me before.
