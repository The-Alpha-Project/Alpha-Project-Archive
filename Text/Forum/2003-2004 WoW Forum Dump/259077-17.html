There seemed to be some confusion as to the difference between the 'Moving PvP zone' idea by myself and the original idea 'Dynamic PvP zones' by Bladefist...

As stated before this 'Dynamic zone' would take place near beaches and borders and such. But there would also be a 'normal zone' that would function as a standard PvP area where you could PK, fight 1on1 against other players, or go creeping.

The main difference is that the 'Dynamic' would be more of a large scale battle. Also, you may not be able to tackle people in your own faction (IE: you probably won't have a human fighting another human). And, this zone would open up in different places and close in others.

The 'Normal' area is a standard type of pvp; that's obvious. But why not make this area move as well?...

I really like the ideas behind this system, and really hope Blizz has something similar in mind.

I have one issue that I'm not happy about, and some possible solutions for it. My problem is the thought of only being able to play chars of one faction on a server. This won't fly for me, I'll want to play my Human Mage when my alliance loving friends are playing, but when they aren't around I'm going to be all about my orc warlord.

I agree that direct communication between players on opposing factions is not needed, however I still want to be able to see my friend when he logs onto his account. I have 2 solutions for this problem.

1.) A timer. If you are involved in any FvF play, you may not log onto a char of an opposing faction for a set time. Like 30 mins or an hour. If the system works as purposed, after the decision was made of what area was to be attacked it would be made public knowledge anyhow. It's not like you could learn anything helpful by logging into a char of the other faction. A timer would prevent any 'immediate' switching of sides in the heat of the battle.

2.) Realm server tiering. This would work as follows. You create an account regionaly, similar to D2, for example USEast. Then you have the choice between 3 or 4 servers in that region. You would have a freind list that worked across all the servers in that region (just to view logging, not necessarily being able to talk). That way I would know when my alliance loving freinds logged on, so I could switch servers. This would defiantly be more technically daunting than a timer option.

In short I'm going to be pissed off if I don't have a way to see when my friends log on (to an opposing faction) so I can join up with them, but I won't be bothered at all if I can't talk to them while playing an opposing faction.
