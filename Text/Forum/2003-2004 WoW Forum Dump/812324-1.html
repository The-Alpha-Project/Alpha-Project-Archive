1. EQ2 Features | 12/3/2003 11:13:12 AM GMTST
This list is for those who are unfamiliar with Everquest II's unique features:

1.Character Transfer Payment Program: Unlike the original Everquest, Everquest II will offer a payment program for transfering characters to different servers. A twelve month $24.99 installment plan does not go into effect until the transfer has been completed.

2.Rent: Annoyed with other online "virtual" worlds that make you pay your hard-earned gold for that tiny little abode? In Everquest II that is no longer an issue. For $39.99 a month you can inhabit a luxury two-bedroom, two-bath estate with furnishings and decor you select from a variety of shops located throughout the World of Norath (prices on these items has yet to be determined).

3.Auto-Naming Subscription Service: Tired of the name you've had since day one? Well not any longer. For $19.99 a month this service will allow you to wash-away your sins. *Six month minimum subscription. $99.99 early termination fee.

4.Loot Bumping: Does it seem like you are always getting the same thing from those beetles and snakes? Well, for a small charge of $9.99* you will have a chance to "bump" that loot to something better**. *Charge per mob. **No guarantee on items after using this service.

5.Insta-Guild Simulation Service: Can't get into that High-level guild? Do you constantly leave your computer making others upset? Well that will never be a problem with this new Everquest II feature. For $199.99 a month you can have up to thirty "Server-Controlled" characters at your disposal. They will fight, chat, level, and even share the loot as you fight your way to victory lane.

6.Adult Emote Programming Service: Bored sending unexciting kisses to that virtual hottie? For $29.99 a month you will be privileged to eighty-two distinct sexual emotes*. This feature will allow you to be with that special friend in ways other players could only dream about. For a small fee of just $29.99 you can rent a virtual room inside our many hotels located throughout Norath. *All sexual emotes must be used inside a designated hotel facility.

7.Map-it Service: This unique $19.99 a month service will allow the user to bring up a map to the areas he or she has explored throughout Norath. We, however, have taken it one step further. For an additional charge of only $49.99 you'll also be privileged to 24 zones completely revealed for you to explore.

8.Ad-Go-Away Service: Do those new Coke, Tide, and Nike pop-up adds annoy you as you make your way through Norath? Well they won't with this service. For $24.99 a month you can play without the temptations of our new Everquest sponsors.

9.Ignore-List Service:Due to the usage of this feature from the original Everquest, we have decided to implement a much stronger version for Everquest II. Not only can you still put somebody on your ignore list, but this service will automatically tell you everything that person says. And the price of this feature: Free. We value our customers.

10:Auto-Expansion Update Service:Gone are the days of going to the store or ordering the latest expansions to Everquest II. With this $39.99 a month service we will automatically download all expansions directly to your computer. But this service doesn't just stop there! We will also start all new services* introduced through the new expansion packs. All automatically downloaded to you for your convenience. *All new services will be charged separately.


As you can see, Everquest II is shaping-up to be everything the original Everquest was, and a whole lot more.

14. Re: EQ2 Features | 12/3/2003 3:26:35 PM GMTST
There is a lot of speculation about EQ2 and I have not read most of the interviews talking about what is planned.

But EQ2 is introducing some neat things.

EQ2 Weaknesses:

Class system. There are too many classes planned, so many are skeptical how this will work out and how appealing this really is.

Graphics. Detailed but lack style and atmosphere.

Some EQ2 Innovations of note:

Guild Fellowships. Fellowships are guilds of "6 members or less" that are meant for friends. To address playability, all members of a fellowship share in a common experience point pool whether they are all online or not to make sure their level advancement is synchronized.

Assets. Guilds can own property for the purposes of guild halls etc. Players can own and run ships. I think this has aesthetic appeal.

Economic sophistication. To control deflation or item proliferation their will be decay for most items. The first time an item is sold to another player it is susceptible to decay.

Trade Raids. They actually have plans for a raid that involves crafting - not fighting. In this case tradesman that band togoether can build a structure or make something special.

And the list goes on - I am not following EQ2 very closely. I know most of you zealots lack either the brains or the level headedness to appreciate the strengths of other gamings systems - but in the event a neuron is firing among you today you might give credit where it is due to another product for a change.

I presume the pricing on the various issues in the original post is a joke. If you declare otherwise - provide the link.
