25. Re: Warriors, BAH! | 11/27/2003 7:08:12 PM GMTST
Okay so far it appears to be the consensus that warrior classes do not take much wit, independent of the issue that some folks feel Paladins make better tanks.

Based on EQ (if you don't like this reference that's fine, use you're own and offer counter speculation) a high level warrior had these concerns:

1. Optimizing gear choices for optimal hate. To illicit & hold aggro this meant figuring out that fast attacks, good procs and then damage per second were important (in the EQ system - not likely relevant to WoW).

2. Judging how much time you needed to attack a major mob to build sufficient aggro (hate). Tanks need to keep the mob attacking them before giving the call for others to join the fight (nukers, rogues etc.), otherwise they lose aggro instantly. This time varies with the power of the mob.

3. Positioning the mob so that it's rear was easily accessible to melee classes. Not always easy, since if pets were involved they had to be catered to since they do not move around the mob itself easily. In corridoors or indoor battles keeping the mobs off walls so it does not fall out of range of rogues can be a real challenge. The fighter in controlling aggro has to move to correct this (or not move etc.) but to be careful to not to move too much or lose aggro completely.

4. Paying attention to Mob actions (messages). In EQ if the mob 'enraged' you recieved double ripost damage if you were fighting from the front. At this point you stop hitting, and just taunt. If you miss this message you go down quick.

5. When to activate special disciplines that increased your durability. Not a big issue, but this gets tricky when working with a secondary tank, where the duo passes mob aggro when the discipline wears off.

6. Sacraficing your second attack (off hand) in tough tanking situations. I am still working with this, but if you have aggro and the healing is still having trouble, it is possible to swap a shield for you second weapon. This increases your durability but puts you at risk of losing aggro.

7. Group discipline. Often players are so anxious to get in there and 'do damage' the fighter may not have enough time to build aggro before losing it to another party member. For easy encounters this is no biggy - for high level encounters it is a disaster. Getting group(s) to watch and listen to tank messages before joining in on the fight can be a real challenge in party discipline and coordination.

8. Understanding the difference between a rooted or mezzed mob before selecting the group's next attack target. While rooted mobs can be attacked instantly, mezzed mobs should be taunted several times before attacking by the warrior, so that when their mez is broken they do not run straight for the enchanter and kill him.

Again all of this comes from my tanking days in EQ (I was a priest in Shadowbane). Because aggro in WoW is just the click of a button - much more reliable and simpler than in EQ - if it works the way the taunt does for Stormgiants for Night elve units in WCIII - this will be simpler in WoW.

So based on a simpler taunt system in WoW (speculation), this could mean class that has been dumbed down considerably in this area. So while providing fighters with more options in some respects in WoW perhaps Blizzard at the same time has given them less to think about.
