About de-allocation:

Without total deallocation I, and many others, will be reluctant to venture with our skill points. In D II I usually waited until I was level 10+ to spend skill points because I had no idea what tree I wanted and didnt want to risk my limited skill points.

However if you allow total de-allocation than you can just shift yer points around whenever you want to do something new.

If you do a partial deallocation (give up 5 points to gain 4 or whatever) people will be more willing to venture, but powergamers and people who want to play to their full potential will still refuse to, to preserve that 1 in 5 skill points =X

My idea:
You have X skill points to deallocate over the course of your char's lifetime. Once you have spent you "deallocation points" then you are stuck with whatever you have. This is flexible enough to allow for small venturing, or the changing of your main tradeskill maybe once. However, it is rigid enough so you can't go swapping your points around whenever you need to do something you dont have the points for.
