The Gravesite of Cenarius

Races:Undead
Difficulty: Very Hard
Suggested players: 4-12
Class: Any (Prefferably magic users)

Undead Quest

It is said that there are untold treasures and powers that await to be taken. Calculating and treacherous, you hastily decide to venture with this man. It takes you days to reach Kalimdor by travelling on mounts and by ship, until you Ashenvale Forest. When you enter this forest you are suddenly attacked by Night Elf archers. You enter the tomb only to be attacked by the same Night Elves. You fight valiantly and you manage to fend off the pesky elves. You are nearly at Cenarius's tomb, as you enter you see endless piles of gold and gems. The man says not to touch anything until he gives the signal but it is too late. Another member of your party already pocketed a ruby and suddenly the ground shakes violently. There are worms in the tomb that act as guardiand and the begin to flood the tomb from behind. The mage in your group manages to freeze the wall, but you are now sealed in. The man says that he has what he wants and now you could help yourselves to whatever you wanted. He then dissapears.... *available only to Warlocks.

In Jaedenar (in Felwood), you encounter an orc Warlock named Hraglaj Blacktongue, attempting to resurrect the Shadow Council. If you are orc, you are allowed into Jaedenar whether or not you complete this quest. Gnomes, humans, and undead must do this or they won't be allowed into the outpost.

Here, take this medallion, and show it to the sea captain Nagthork. She will take you to the Broken Isles, where you will find Drak'thul, and remove the Head of Cho'gall from him, by any means necessary."

If you're anyone but orc, he finishes with:
"Do this, and I will allow you to walk freely in Jaedenar."

Now, Nagthork Bloodener would be the captain of a ship that can transport anyone between the two continents, but, if you have this quest and approach her, you can show her the medallion, and she would say:

If you're going to take my ship out of business for a while, the least you can do is make yourself useful."

So you set sail and arrive on the Broken Isles, and hack your way through this and that, and finally find Drak'thul's hut, with Drak'thul standing outside.

"So, the Shadow Council has sent some lackey to do what they could not? Well, if you're stupid enough to follow their lead, there's obviously no point in reasioning with you. But know this, novice, I shall be no simple foe."

"The Gypsies Stole My Child!"

Level: Intermediate
Time: 2 hours
Background: There is a band of travelling Gypsies who travel from town to town. They offer services such as horse training, purchase and sale of jems and jewellery, fortune telling and gossip, as well as sale of rogue class equipment and weapons. They are basically a band of rogues and also do pickpocketing and other theft when they visit a town.
Description: This quest is offered only to rogues. When you pass through a town recently visited by the Gypsies a lady comes up to you with the quest "!" above her head. "Oh Sir please help me! A band of Gypsies just passed through this town and they stole my only daughter! The rogues must be far from here now, but I heard they were heading to the town of Trammel. If you travel quickly you may catch them up and possibly retrieve my daughter! I hear you are a master of stealth and also very handy with the dagger and short sword. You will need all your skills to rescue my child from these scum, but if you help me I will be forever greatful!"
Development: When you catch up to the Gypsies they will be camped and have scouts on look out. The gypsies (who are rogues) have pretty good stealth detection, so getting to the child will not be easy. Killing all the gypsies won't be a realistic option, unless you bring many allies with you. When you finally make your way to the caravan in which the girl is resting you speak with her and discover that she is ambivalent about returning to her parents. An old gypsie offers you a reward and promise of additional aid in the future if you leave the child with them. If you try to escape with the child the alarm will be raised. You now have a choice.
Rewards: Upon returning the child to the mother - 10 gold, as much supplies as you can carry (bread, pies, wine, cold meats etc.) plus a cape of stealth enhancement (rare). If you leave the child with the Gypsies - they will give you a high quality poison dagger (rare), will offer you some specialist rogue skills training and also offer you a new quest. What you don't know is that also upon returning the girl to her parents you will be introduced to a rogue skills trainer in town, who also has neat skills to teach.

The idea of this quest is to give a rogue character the opportunity to show their true nature and make an alliance with one rogue faction or another (the reputation system). The rogue faction represented by the Gypsies is like a "neutral" faction, and if you ally with them they will offer lots of interesting "neutral" type rogue quests revolving around stealing valuable stuff and bringing it to them. They will offer useful skills training to you over time as your reputation with them improves.

If you decide to fight your way out and return the child to her parents your reputation with the Gypsies will obviously suffer, but you will gain an introduction to the rogue faction based in the town. This rogue faction is a "Robbin Hood" type group, basically "good" with a "steal from the rich give to the poor" type motto. You will be able to do lots of cool quests with them and get good skills training from them if you choose to go down this path. Obviously there will be another rogue faction in the area which is "evil" and you will be able to seek them out too if you want to. ><

214. Re: List your quest ideas for Bliz. | 1/25/2004 2:02:58 PM GMTST
Ok, here is an idea I used in my last WFRP scenario, but anyone can feel free to use it:

"Werewolf Curse"

Level: advanced
Time: 30 min to 1,5 hour
Description:

Step 1: Several farmers are worried about their cattle feeing slaughtered by a strange beast recently. If a player speaks to one of them (farmer Markson), they will say that apparently, something evil is lurking in the nearby woods. The rumor says someone is under an evil curse which had turned him into a werewolf. The farmer will say that local noble, baron William asked a local druid, Burnhoof to investigate that matter and Burnhoof declined help and was seen very angry leaving the William's palace. Farmer Markson will give players direction where to find Burnhoof.

Step 2: Upon speaking with Burnhoof he will reveal following: yes, there is a werewolf roaming in the area. And this werewolf is no one other than sir William's only son, John. Burnhoof asked the spirits to reveal the origin of the curse and the spirits told him following story: About a month ago, John was hunting solo in the woods. When he was deep in the forests, he found a beautiful naked girl, gathering herbs. He immediately fell in love in her, and proposed to marry her. She declined, and John tried to take her by force. A short fight started and John killed the girl. The girl happened to be a daughter of a Esma, a powerful witch. Esma found John pulling sword out of her daughter's heart and placed a very powerful curse on John, turning him into a werewolf.

Hearing such a story Burnhoof thought the witch did a right thing, therefore declined to help William. However, now he witnessed the trouble the werewolf is causing and he is not that sure he made the right choice, so he can help player to solve the problems.

As he knows, there are 3 ways to unmade the curse:
A. Kill the werewolf - which will end the farmers problems
B. Kill the witch - which will end the curse and free John
C. Find Esma and convince her to unmake the curse.

The rest of quest depends on player's decoction.

Step 3: Killing the werewolf
This is easiest solution. You just need to wait till night then find and kill the beast. He will roam in the woods (the werewolf is trying to find Espa to kill her to end the curse, but he is unable to find her). Werewolf is quite tough and may require help of another player or two
Upon killing the beast, the player can return to Markson for reward.

Step 4: Killing the witch
This is a bit more complicated. The witch lives in a hut deep in the forest, and the hut is guarded by a summoned guardian (zombie for example). The players need to kill the zombie, then kill the witch. Espa is quite a powerful witch and will require a full party.
Upon killing the witch, the player can return to Markson for a reward, additionally, sir William can give them a nice reward as well.

Step 5: Changing the curse
This is the most complicated solution. You need to get to the witch (sneak past the guardian, not killing him - if you kill him, the witch wont talk to you, you can try again next day). Then you need to talk with Espa. She is unwilling to lift the curse, however, hearing there are innocent people suffering, and those people are losing their daughters as well, she can change the curse into something less dangerous, weretoad for example. However, she needs few things to do it: firstly, she needs to make a potion to change the curse. She needs a rare herb (found by a herbalist or bought) and a magical toad heart to do it. Magical toad is a creature find nearby, about same difficulty as the werewolf. Then she needs players to incapacitate the werewolf (she gives player an oil, which will do the job - then player have to find and fight beast as in step 3). Upon incapacitating the werewolf, Espa appears, does her magic and werewolf is changed into a weretoad. Player then may talk with Espa for a reward, then with farmer Markson for another reward.

Reward:
Depending on the course of actions, player will get few rewards. He (she) will always get the farmer Markson reward - which might be a bag of gold + some magic item. If they chosen to kill Espa, sir William can give them another reward, quite powerful - like a magic shield. If they have chosen to change the curse, they beside Markson reward, they get another reward from Espa - a magic wand or something like that.
