"Babysitting"

Level: Beginning

Time: 15 minutes

Description: Pity the poor seamstress -- she has more children than she can keep track of. Frazzled to the point of fraying, she needs a break, even if just for a few minutes. That's where you come in -- you must watch over the little monsters for fifteen minutes while the seamstress heads off to the tavern for a little nip o' courage.

The children (at least half a dozen) are all in a large room with 4-6 doors. They run around constantly like overcaffeinated hyperactive ADHD speed freaks. Any time one of them reaches a door, they begin to pick the lock. Your job is to travel from door to door shooing them away and relocking the door. If one of them stays unattended at a door too long, it will open and all the children will pour out into the streets and beyond -- quest over, you fail. If you can manage to keep them contained for 15 minutes, the seamstress will return and reward you.

Reward: Training and crafting ingredients from the seamstress, coin, exp, a rare tailoring recipe, and/or one free child (them's good eatin').

"Out of Time"

Level: Advanced

Time: 30 minutes

Description: A magical experiment to manipulate time itself has gone horribly, horribly awry. The result: time has fractured at the location of the experiment. Worse still, the anomaly is growing! You must stop this menace by destroying the Dark Hourglass in the magical laboratory. Any unprotected mortal subjected to these temporal forces would be instantly killed. The good news is that we have a special amulet that can shield you from these fantastic forces. The bad news is that while wearing the amulet, your own speed will be cut in half. Putting on the amulet requires a mystical ceremony right here that only I can perform, and it cannot be removed until the Hourglass is destroyed. In the meantime, you will move at half speed, fight at half speed, cast and half speed, etc.

Agreeing to this quest drops the player into slow-motion. Finishing the quest as quickly as possible is probably a VERY good idea. While the travel, combat, and completion of the quest won't be TOO difficult, adjusting to the temporary change in game physics will be tricky enough to make this quest quite interesting. Of course, it will be short so as not to be too frustrating.

Reward: This quest could open up access to a new high-level area behind the laboratory or through a portal in the laboratory. Similarly, the quest could free high-level wizards who will show their gratitude to the player by offering quests; training; new spells, items, and rare alchemical ingredients to buy; or the standard items-exp-and-faction bonuses that quests so commonly give.

Ok, here's the final part of my "breadcrumb" quest (the first part is on page 2 of this... it's not very good, but it leads up to this).

Level: Master
Group size: 1

While in the wilderness on another quest, you are suddenly ambushed by a group of mercenaries. Their leader, an armored warrior bearing an oddly familiar symbol, glares at you, then exclaims:

"I finally found you, monster. I hope you're ready to pay for all you have done!"

Without giving you much chance to respond, he and his men attack. It is a rather hard fight, since you are outnumbered, but as you should be pretty high level (there's a minimal level at which they will first appear), you have a good chance to win. If you die, you will meet them again, eventually, in a random location.

Once you kill the bloodthirsty Knight, you find on his body an Order for your death, signed by a noble you know quite well (having done many quests for him in the past). Since you remember leaving him on good terms, your mission is to learn why he wants you dead.

The first step is, naturally, to get there. NPCs have suddenly begun acting very distrustful of you. Some guards attack you on sight. Wanted posters with your face appear on tavern walls the world throughout!... Clearly, this is very distracting.

Once you enter the Noble's home, the guards attack you mercilessly. After making your way to the noble's room, the old man, stuttering with rage, attempts to kill you himself.

After you defeat him, you learn that the old psycho firmly believes that you stormed into his home several months ago, murdering his only daughter. You must escape from the building quickly... and the only way out is through the very room where the murder took place.

The room reeks of blood... How can it, if this murder happened so long ago?... Before making an escape through the window, you pick up your first clue about the one who framed you... a small shard of broken glass.

Your mission changes. Now hunted by guards, bountyhunters, and who knows what else, you must solve this mystery, and clear your name.

However, it isn't a hard puzzle. The shard you picked up glows slightly, guiding you West... Along the way, you fight many more bounty hunters... and a mysterious hooded warrior. This man proves to be every bit your equal, but retreats before the fight becomes serious. He leaves another shard of glass behind...

You are driven further and further by these glimmering charms. You reach a small port town on the western shore. There, upon seeing the shards you hold, an old fisherman gives you a third, and tells you he has been paid a great sum of money to take you to a removed island...

...

It is there that you come face to face with the spirit of the Demonic mirror... now a mirror image of yourself, only dressed in dark plate, and carrying a demonic sword. That was the true nature of the Dreadlord's gift. The Priestess was not corrupted: she was slain and replaced. Now, you, the one who broke the Mirror, shall be the thing's form. Its purpose is to pave the way for a third invasion of Demons. In order to do this, however, it must kill the original owner of its current shape. Then, it will have reached its true power.

Your face is about to become the face of the next Demonic Weapon to be unleashed on Azeroth. It is up to you, and you alone, to stop the dark reflection of your soul...

You are up against Your own Self, only with slightly different gear. Your skills are almost identical, as are your abilities and stats. His AI is as good as the game allows. He has plenty of healing items on him... He is, simply put, your ultimate enemy.

Can you defeat him?...

"Running Circles"

"Running Circles II"

Level - Begginer.
Estimated time to complete - 15 minutes after night fall.
Quest - The farmer offers another reward for you, if you will follow these bandits back to thier camp, and stop them from stealing his sheep.
OutcomeA - The player follows the bandits back to thier camp, and slays them all, then returns with what's left of the farmers sheep.
OutcomeB - The player follows the bandits back to thier camp, and talks the bandits into giving the sheep back, and to stop stealing them(Perhaps multiple choices which I've seen in other MMOs where the right choice gets you what you need.)
OutcomeC - The player follows the bandits back to thier camp, and takes the bandits offer to send a 'message' to the farmer, that the bandits will do what they want when they want.
RewardA - Farmer gives up coin and some type of item, obviously something that a farmer could give... Meat, a little coin, etc.
RewardB - Farmer gives up coin and some type of item, the item could be better than the RewardA item since there was no killing involved. And the player would bring the sheep back by doing it this way.
RewardC - Coin, a weapon maybe, and a new quest line.

"No Better Bandit Than Me"

Level - Begginer.
Estimated time to complete - 15 minutes
Quest - Since the player helped the bandits, they now ask that the player help take care of a few loose ends. The bandits ask that the player infiltrate a rival bandit groups camp, and steal a jewel.
OutcomeA - Player infiltrates the camp, and steals the jewel while the bandits sleep.
OutcomeB - Player is caught by the bandits, and comes back empty handed.
RewardA - Player is rewarded with a weapon, or item, or coin, and gets to keep whatever else they stole.
RewardB - Player is attacked by all the bandits, and cannot continue to do quests for them.

Note - The quests from these bandits could go on and on, and eventually get tougher, allowing the player to return to them later to do more quests. Lot of things that the great minds at Blizzard can do with this one.
