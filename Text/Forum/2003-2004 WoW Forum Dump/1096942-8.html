*** Sheesh! - took out a lot of stuff, and this is still way long! Well, you asked, Kat!

I'll lump all the muds I've played into one group, and say that I enjoyed their complexities, but wanted some kind of graphic interface.

EQ Likes: Played this WAY too much. I liked that it was engaging, and a lot of work to do something, and I like the ability to play with real-life friends (but I found that I spent most of my time doing solo stuff -soloed a Paladin to 56 -ouch!

EQ Dislikes:
Questing - The questing system left a lot to be desired. Not enough information in-game on where to get them and what needed to be done. Also, you could collect the items while not even on the quest, and start & end the quest after you had done all the work.

Items - Equipment was the rule. After a while everything came down what equipment you had, and the ONLY way to get the good stuff was to group & raid and that just wasn't my playstyle.

AntiSocial - Too many people were exploiting the looting system, or training mobs into groups just because they could, or because they wanted to mess with people.

Tradeskills - It was far, far, too expensive to increase in a tradeskill. The only people that could benefit from the system was someone in a guild with their guildmates donating money & items. Also, the ONLY desireable crafted items were the high-skill ones- the expansion pack items were better than crafted and easier to get.

Customer Service - Always got the feeling when dealing with GMs that it was my word against someone else's, and I usually got the fuzzy end of the lollipop, even when the other party was clearly in the wrong. Also, most of the GMs that I interacted with were not as experienced as I was and gave me more attitude than help.

DAoC Likes: Played this way too much also, starting with beta. I like a lot about this game. The quest/task system, the looting systems, the RvR play. I seriously wonder why I am not playing this still. In all the time that I played, I never had to interact with a GM.

Shadowbane Likes: I enjoyed beta here because the game had SO much potential, and the devs were actually talking with us during the process. I like just about everything about this, and still play from time to time.

Shadowbane DisLikes: My friends were disappointed with the movement style. They wanted something more like EQ or FPS games. I didn't really care, but it kept my friends from playing and me playing with them. My other quibble is that there isn't any questing- if there were, I would be playing a lot more.

Asheron's Call Dislikes: It's from Microsoft, and honestly I never gave it much of a chance because of that. Also, their approach to solving issues was a joke (take Portal Storms as an example). Aside from that, it wasn't different enough from EQ to lure me away.

Earth & Beyond: Mission system had some serious problems, and movement outside the spaceship was primitive and not customizeable. I wanted very much for this to be a better game to play.

SWG Likes: Finally a sci-fi MMO based on a well-known franchise! Lots of potential here! Oh, and becoming a Jedi was possible, Uber, and difficult!

SWG Dislikes: Never got the feeling that the Dev team knew what they wanted the game to be. Got the feeling they were trying to please too many people, and didn't have a clear idea of the type of gameplay they wanted.

All Fantasy-Based MMORPGs: I've been disappointed in the way that so many MMOs have stuck very closely to Tolkein for building their universe. I understand the reasons behind this better than most people, but it still is a disappointment. When I first looked at info on WoW, it seemed that Blizzard was finally breaking away from that, but it didn't happen. As an example, I have never been able to be a dwarven druid. I hear your argument that it doesn't make sense, but you're basing that on Tolkein's stories, or someone else's rules based on Tolkein. How about a little more open-mindedness? How about a little more freedom?

Likes:

[ul]Content. Any kind of content. By far the most important feature in a MMORPG. In any MMORPG that I've quit in less then a month *COUGH*E&B*COUGH* it was because of a lack of content.

  • Specifically, I like to have an atmosphere of having a lot of things to do - which is why I'm so interested in WOW, becaus it looks like the devs are putting a lot into it. Quests are a great example of what I like. They have rewards, they're different every time, and they make me feel like I'm interacting positivly with the gameworld.

  • Interesting and unique spells/skills. The second biggest thing in a MMORPG for me - and another reason why I hated E&B, but quite liked, say...AO. The more skills/spells, the better. They don't all have to be uber useful - god knows most of the EQ mages summoning spells were junk...but they were still a lot of fun. I think diversity is more interesting then the same spell 20 times, even if that spell is more effective.

  • Good systems for player interaction. Reducing griefing, allow people to group with their friends and make guild forming easy. SWG and EQ both made it far to difficult to form a guild, IMO.

  • Things to see and discover. I LOVE exploring in game worlds, and not being able to guess what I'm going to find. EQ did OK with this - SWG was terrible at it.

    Bad things:

  • Empty space. Both EQ and SWG suffered from this - especially SWG. I got the overall impression that SWG had just used a random terrain generator, and then went through and smoothed out any mistakes it had made. I like to feel like each area of the game was hand crafted, and I think it makes for a better game if they take the time to do that. At the very least, areas should have things in them, ALWAYS. NPCs, monsters, whatever - the big problem with SWG is that you had A)Big, empty cities. None of the NPCs talked, none were interesting, it was like a ghost town. B)Big, empty wilderness. They had a HUGE gameworld in SWG. And all they did was cover it in random monster spawns. There would be perhaps one thing to see in an area square of 5 min of walking time. The rest was just textured land, with groups of mobs randomly scattered in it. BOOOORING.

  • Griefers. KSers, quest item ninja looters, PvP TKers, etc. The bottom line is I dislike being around people, that are, frankly, jerks. I'm glad WoW has a lot of systems to help solve this stuff.

  • The same mob at every level. Changing the color of a mob's skin and giving a new ability is still boring. A beetle is a beetle no matter if it's blue, brown, or orange. Now, a beetle, then a giant beetle, then a giant flying beetle, then some huge queen beetle...that I can live with.

  • Unbalance. I hate it when the class I like to play is never invited to groups, because it's considered "worthless."
