What is fishing?

As you'd expect this allows a character to hone their hunting skills and enables you to fish for fish and other things lurking in the watery depths.

How do I become a Fisherman?

You need to locate a Trainer and have at least 5 skill point spare.  Both Trainers and Suppliers can be found in the following locations:

Fishing Trainer Locations

Stormwind : Arnold Leland, Jetty, Trade District.
Ironforge : Grimnor Stonebeard, 'Traveling Fishman', {#3}

Fishing Supplier Locations

Stormwind :  Catherine Leland,  Jetty, Trade District.
Ironforge : Tansy Buddlefizz, 'Traveling Fishman', {#3}

You train to three levels of proficiency and each has certain prerequisites that you need to meet before you are able to progress to the next level of proficiency.

How do I fish?

 

Fishing
Icon

Bobber
Watching

After you have learnt fishing you will need to buy a fishing rod from a Supplier.  To fish you will need to equip it, just like a weapon. Your weapon will need to be put in your inventory.  To cast you right click on the fishing icon {left} that would have been placed in your Abilities screen when you first learnt Fishing.  It's best to drag this to your action bar so it's easily accessible.  A fishing bobber will be cast at some random location within the water you are standing near.  A small timer gauge will sit at the bottom of the screen, just as it does when you're casting some spells and this is how long that cast will last for.  

Hold you cursor over the bobber and wait.  When you see a splash around the bobber right click on it and the catch will either get away or you will be successful and a loot box will pop up showing what you have caught.  Your skill in fishing can rise even if your catch gets away.

You can't do much else while fishing as you need to watch the bobber with your cursor hovering over it waiting for a splash so this skill is only for those with patience and time to kill.

How can I improve my fishing?

As well as purchasing a better pole from a Fishing Supplier you can also apply imbues from items to further improve your chances of catching something.  These items sit in your inventory and you right click to activate them.  The Shiny Bauble applied to this Fishing Pole on the left added +25 to the fishing skill level  for 10 minutes.  The other passive attribute you see there came with the pole.

Also you will need to fish in areas appropriate for your fishing skill level.  If you fish in areas that are too high a level for you you will not be as successful. You can judge this by the number of unsuccessful casts you have.  If you're not catching anything then move onto an easier area.  It's best to start your fishing career just around your start town.  You will catch different types of fish and items in different areas.

What can I catch and what use are the things I catch?

Not just fish thankfully, however fish are good for health regeneration so you can dish these out to your party members {they are better than fish bought at Vendors} or sell them back to vendors. More interestingly you can hook chests, gems, pearls and even quests. Some things such as Oily Blackmouth fish can be used with Alchemy to make potions.  Other fish can be used with the trade skill Cooking.

To recap on the Fishing basics:

  • Training costs Skill Points
  • Fishing equipment and paraphernalia {bait} costs money
  • As well as fish {can also be used with Cooking Trade Skill} you can catch chests {some locked and need Locking Picking Trade Skill}, quests, gems, pearls {can be used in Blacksmithing, Tailoring & Engineering} and other equipment
  • Bait {bonus items} lasts for a duration not indefinitely
  • Costs no mana/rage/health to cast
  • Fishing poles need to be equipped in your primary weapon hand
  • Your skill in Fishing can rise even if the catch gets away
