What is Alchemy?

Alchemy is the ability to turn herbs into healing and other types of potions.

How do I become an Alchemist?

You need to locate a Trainer and have at least 1 skill point spare.  Both Trainers and Suppliers {of reagents/ingredients} can be found in the following locations:

Alchemy Trainer Locations

Stormwind : Lilyssia Nightbreeze, 'Alchemy Needs', Mage District
Elwynn Forest: Alchemist Mallory, right of entrance to Stormwind city up small valley in the hills
Ironforge : Tally Berryfizz, Commerce Ward {# 2}
Undercity : Apothecary Quarter {# 30}
ThunderBluff : Bena Winterhoof  

Alchemy Supplier Locations

Stormwind : Maria Lumere, 'Alchemy Needs', Mage District
Ironforge : Soolie Berryfizz, Commerce Ward {# 2}
Undercity : Apothecary Quarter {# 30}

You train to three levels of proficiency and each has certain prerequisites that you need to meet before you are able to progress to the next level of proficiency.

How do I get Ingredients?

You will be best to use the Herbalist skill which you can train in also.  This will allow you to find plants for yourself in the wilderness and gather them.  Other components can be brought from a Herbalist Supplier. You'll need various containers and these are the people who will sell you them.  You can also trade with other players for ingredients.

How do I make potions?

You click on the icon on the left to open your Alchemy recipe book. To become the ultimate Alchemist you will need to obtain recipes from a trainer, find recipes from quests, killing monsters or from another player. Grey recipes are trivial, green ones are average, yellow ones are above average and orange ones are the hardest. Just because the recipe is hard it doesn't mean you are going to fail, it simply means that the chance of getting your skill to increase when creating is more difficult. Your skill will only increase if you work on green, yellow or orange recipes. The most difficult of recipes will need to be followed in an Alchemist's Lab in order to work. Potions to heal or add resistances, provide mana or invisibility are possible as well as oils to treat armours.

If you work on any of the trivial recipes you won't go up in skill at all. This way the player won't have to endure endless attempts at creating a potion before success, they will always create the potion but won't always gain points in the skill.

The Alchemy recipe book displays the ingredients you have and the next figure how many you need to make that potion. If any of the components icons are greyed out it's because you do not have any of them. If you have all the ingredients simply click 'create' and it will automatically create the potion and move it to your backpack. If you have one of the same item already there it will stack the new item on top of it.

As your Alchemy skill increases, the recipes will open up at an Alchemist Trainer. If you go back to a town there's more than likely an Alchemist Trainer there who will teach you recipes in return for money. Some potions (such as the Troll Blood Potion) are from recipes awarded after completing a quest, simply right clicking on the recipe scroll to learn and add it to your recipe book. If you've already learned that recipe you can always trade the scroll to another player who hasn't but you can't give your recipes to someone else once it's in your recipe book.

Having trained as an Apprentice Alchemist you start with three recipes in your Alchemy recipe book ready to learn, the top three in this table.  From there on down the list you need to be a higher level Alchemist to create the potions.  The more potions you make the higher your level of proficiency in Alchemy will rise so to start off with you're going to be making lots of the top three potions but as you can give them to your friends it'll at least give you a chance to win friends and influence people.

To recap on the Alchemy basics:

  • Training costs Skill Points
  • Recipes cost money
  • Recipes come in grades.  Grey = trivial, green = average, yellow = above average and orange the hardest.
  • Following a recipe will never fail.
  • Following trivial recipes will not improve your skill level in Alchemy
  • Following the other grade of recipes has more chance of increasing your skill level in Alchemy relative to the grade of recipe, hardest recipes having the most chance to increase your level. 
  • You can give potions to friends to use or trade
  • You can not give recipes to players once it's in your Alchemy recipe book.
