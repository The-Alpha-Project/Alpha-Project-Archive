What is Enchanting?

Enchanting is the ability to imbue items with magical and physical enhancements, temporarily or permanently.  Enchanters do not create the original item.  There are two types of enhancements applied to items; Enchantments which are permanent and Imbues which, while usually more powerful, are temporary and one of each {Enchantment & Imbue} can be on a single item at one time.

How do I become an Enchanter?

You will need to train with an Enchanting Trainer and listed below are the known locations of Trainers and Suppliers of ingredients.

What materials do I need and how do I get them?

Enchanters need herbs and other components to complete recipes. They will also need a Runed Copper Rod and a Runed Silver Rod for certain enchantments which they can get from a blacksmith and then enchant it. The Rods are not exhaustible so you don't have to keep buying them. Enchanters can obtain the herbs from merchants, other players or Train in Herbalism to collect them themselves which is probably the most efficient and convenient method.

Can I Enchant/Imbue other people's Items?

You can enchant and imbue other people's items too.  Simply use the Trade window. Get the other person to place the item in the Trade window, perform the enchantment and then hover your cursor over the item to ensure it's Enchanted then hit Trade.  The item will return the other player's backpack/paperdoll.  The other person should also hover their cursor over the item to ensure it's been enhanced before clicking 'Trade' or the item will swap to the Enchanters inventory as it does with a regular trade. Watch for that, as it could be an easy scam for bogus Enchanters offering to do you a favour, relying on you failing to check before you hit Trade. You can not Enchant or Imbue someone else's Soulbound item.

This service means Enchanters can be a valuable part of any team and also charge money for enchanting other people's items.

To recap on some basics of Enchanting

  • One Enchantment and one Imbue can be placed on the same item. Not two of each however
  • Enchantments are permanent
  • Imbues are temporary
  • Casting another Enchantment or Imbue on an item will replace your previous Enchantment or Imbue
  • Enchantments do not stack with other permanent enhancements gained from other trade skills such as the armour kit from Blacksmiths.  Your Enchantment would overwrite the armour kit. It can however stack if the item originally came with the attribute.  Example: If the item came with + to Fire Resist you can still Imbue it with 'Lesser Fire Resistance'.
  • Imbues do not stack with other temporary imbues gained from other skills or trade skills. Example: A Shaman imbuing a weapon with Flametongue Weapon can not then imbue it with Beastslayer and have both imbues on the weapon.  Beastslayer would replace Flametongue.
  • Can Enchant/Imbue other people's items via the Trade window
  • Can not Enchant/Imbue other people's 'Soulbound' items.
  • An Enchanted/Imbued item will not sell for more money at a vendor.
