WarCraft History : The legendary dragons- Alexstrasza, Malygos, Norzdormu, Ysera, and Deathwing- are know as the Five Great Aspects. They are so called for each bears an "aspect" of the world and nature, making each extremely powerful. Each are also the leaders of their own dragon flights (Even though Deathwing and Malygos remain the last of their kind). Originally there were other Aspects, representing various dragon flights; however, they have become extinct. - Source: Legends of Azeroth - Submit FotD

Blizz Nibbles Rush 15:54:PST

With the patch out there's a few bits and pieces from Blizzard of interest.

  • The new downloader has been a nightmare for me and I know others have had problems. However, Blizzard has stated that an alternative solution will be availably,e when the game is released.
  • A couple of bug fixes which include no more fatigue on griffons etc. over water and the PvP (Dis)honorable kill system has been disabled, it wasn't meant to be turned on at the moment.
  • The Caverns of Time dungeon is not complete but will be intended for 50-60+ level characters.
  • Despite thinking we'd be full rested with the "maximum allowable rested bonus", apparently that was a mistake and players receive "rested bonus gained" for the downtime.
  • A few points on raid groups from Rob Pardo confirming a few things including
    • Currently, you can make a raid out of 2 people by clicking the *convert raid* button. We are considering requiring 5 people. These numbers may change.
    • Groups don't get removed from raids, only individuals do.
    • I believe in the current beta, that only the first group to engage will get tapping rights. This is *NOT* intended behavior and will be fixed. The intended behavior is that every group within the raid will get tapping rights which apply to loot, experience and other tapping rules such as polymorph for mages.
    • Drop percentages remain constant. If you kill something with one person or 40 people, the mob has the same percentage chance to drop an item.

  • For those of you experiencing problems such as texture issuews with th enew patch Blizzard suggest resetting your resolution and then switching it back.
  • Auctioneers will be placed back on their boxes for better visibility in-game.

Blizzard to Beta Testers: Take the Weekend Off. onemadogre 16:19:PST

Blizzard has confirmed that they are in the damaged data center.

At 6:19pm EDT yesterday, the datacenter complex in Virginia that is being used to host the World of Warcraft beta test was hit and damaged by a tornado. Because water was leaking into the building in which our servers were located, we shut down the servers as a precautionary measure and flew out a team of our network engineers to the location to survey the area and provide a preliminary assessment.

This morning, we have confirmed that some hardware has taken on water, so we will need to conduct a careful inspection for damage before we can restore power to the servers. This inspection will take 48 hours or longer to complete, so we encourage you to enjoy the rest of your weekend with other activities, as the World of Warcraft beta test will remain offline for at least that long. We will post another update tomorrow afternoon, and we appreciate your continued patience and support.
So go enjoy your weekend. Sure is beautiful outside here where I am sitting.

Server Saga Rush 7:57:PST

This week has been a busy week for WoW news with interviews and the European press tour taking place. The most contentious issue has been servers and the announcment that EU players will not be able to play on North American servers once the game launches. With the North American version launching before the European, this actually makes a lot of sense. Although Blizzard would prefer Europeans to hold on for the EU release (time and money has been spent on a European team and infrastructure), they will be able to buy a North American version assuming they can find a work-around for the billing. Blizzard are hoping for a succesful European launch and this has been illustrated by their financial commitement to the EU WoW community.

In our aticle earlier this week a key word seems to have been ignored which was "initially". What this means to us is the regional server system implemented at launch could change at some point with restrictions removed, probably after all regional versions are on the market and customer demographics are clear.

Not wanting to take any chances, a petition has been set up to encourage Blizzard to remove any restrictions when the game is launched in North America. Going on past experience, petitioning developers makes very little difference, it certainly never did for the many petitions created for Diablo II. We know EU gamers are just ithcing to start playing but we think it's worth holding on, there will be greater benefits in the long term including better support and regional specific content.
