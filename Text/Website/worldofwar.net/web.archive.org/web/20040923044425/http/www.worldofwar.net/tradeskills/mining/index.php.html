What is Mining?

Mining is the excavation of ores, stones and gems from mineral-rich veins which can then be used to create weapons, armour and other items or sell in their raw form to blacksmiths and engineers.

How do I become a Miner?

You seek a Mining Trainer who for the price of skill points will train you as an Apprentice Miner. You can locate the Trainers and Suppliers in the following area.

Mining Trainer Locations

Stormwind : Gelman Stonehand, Dwarven District
Ironforge : Geofram Bouldertoe, Deep Mountain Mining Guild {# 8}
Undercity : War Quarter {# 7}
 Ogrimmar : Red #6
Razor Hill, Durotar : Krunn, Miner

Mining Supplier Locations

Stormwind : Brook Stonebraid, Dwarven District
Ironforge : Golnir Bouldertoe, Deep Mountain Mining Guild {# 8}
Undercity : War Quarter {# 7}
 Ogrimmar : Red #6

You train to three levels of proficiency and each has certain prerequisites that you need to meet before you are able to progress to the next level of proficiency.

You will need a mining pick and also that can be bought from a Suppliers.  You'll keep this in your backpack with you at all times and do not need to equip it to use. 

How do I find Minerals?

Find
Minerals
Icon

Once you have trained as an Apprentice Miner you will find the Rank 1 'Find Minerals' icon on left in your 'Abilities' book.  You should drag this to your action bar ready for use when you're out playing.   Once 'Find Minerals' has been activated ore/stone veins will show up your minimap as yellow dots {right image}. If you hover your cursor over the yellow dot it will tell you the type of mine it is.   Mines can contain more than one mineral, for example a copper mine may also contain some rough stone or Shadowgems.

Veins are located in mountain crevices or jutting out from the side of hills or inside mines/caverns.  Veins can contain minerals such as copper, stone, tin, silver, iron, gold, mithirl, Tigerseye, Shadowgem, Moss Agate and Jade. 

You can print out the following resource maps which will help you locate ore veins to mine.

  • Elwynn Forest
    •
  • Westfall
  • Dun Morogh
  • Loch Modan

The colour of the vein type indicates whether you will gain a Mining skill point when you mine it.  The colour codes are as follows:

  • Grey = No skill increase.
  • Green = Unlikely to get a Mining skill point.
  • Yellow = Probably get a Mining skill point.
  • Orange = Will receive a Mining skill point.
  • Red = Can not mine yet, need a higher skill level in Mining before being able to 

What the vein contains isn't set according to who is Mining it. If it's copper, it's copper for everyone and it will always spawn {reappear} containing the same mineral.

How do I Mine?

Once you have found a vein right click on it to begin mining for minerals.  You will not need to equip your mining pick, your character will automatically swap from their weapon to the pick and begin hacking at the vein.  As explained above you will either receive a Mining skill point when mining the vein or not, depending on your Mining skill level. However, at most you can only ever receive one point per vein but you can mine it maybe four times before it's exhausted of its contents and disappears. It will spawn again in due course, containing the same mineral and you can mine it again if you can be bothered to sit around waiting for it to reappear.

More than one person can mine the vein.  When you've mined it a loot box will appear displaying what has been mined, some copper ore and some stones for example, you just click these as you would any loot and they'll move to your backpack. If you do not move them to your backpack and close the loot window the minerals will return to the vein and someone else can mine for them.

This is a good way for parties of miners to each get a point from a vein as you can take it in turns to mine the vein, get awarded your Mining skill point, do not take the loot and then let the next Miner have a go at the vein and also get a Mining Skill point.  The same as it works in Herbalism when gathering herbs from a bush.

What do I do with the Minerals I have mined?

Smelt
Icon

The ore you Mine will need to be smelt into a usable state and this ability is given to you automatically when you become an Apprentice Miner.  You will need to be by a Forge to smelt.  These are usually located need a Mining Trainer or Supplier {look at the list at the top of the page}.  Once by a Forge {you only need to stand next to it} right click the icon on the left which you'll find in your Abilities page.  This will bring up a list of the smelting recipes you have learnt {recipes cost money from a Mining Trainer}.  You are automatically given the 'Smelt Copper' recipe when you become an Apprentice Miner.  Smelting can also raise your Mining Skill level.

Forges

Forges are usually located near Trainers {look at the list at the top of the page} but occasionally they won't and listed below are such instances.

  • Goldshire : Village shop, opposite Inn
  • Westfall : Moonbrook

The colour of the recipe {in your menu} indicates whether you will gain a skill point in Mining when you create the item.  The colour codes are as follows:

  • Grey = No skill increase.
  • Green = Unlikely to get a Mining skill point.
  • Yellow = Probably get a Mining skill point.
  • Orange = Will receive a Mining skill point.
  • Red = Can not create yet, need a higher skill level in Blacksmithing before being able to

Once you have your ore in a useable form {the bars above} you can use them to create items with your Blacksmithing skill or you can sell the bars to vendors or to other Blacksmiths.

It's advisable to smelt whenever possible as it has storage benefits too.  10 Ore pieces stack in one slot of your backpack whereas you can stack 20 bars in one slot.

To recap on the Mining basics:

  • Training to be a Miner costs Skill Points
  • Learning smelting 'recipes' costs Money
  • Mining works best coupled with Blacksmithing and/or Engineering
  • You can fail when trying to mine a vein. Simply try again.
  • Your skill level in mining will determine which veins you can earn a skill point from {max 1 skill point}.
  • Your skill level in Mining has no effect on what a vein contains.
  • Your skill level in Mining can rise while Smelting.
  • 10 Ore or 20 Bars can stack in one backpack slot.
  • You can mine a vein and not loot the ore to leave for someone else to mine. This is useful for a group of miners to increase their skill in mining.
